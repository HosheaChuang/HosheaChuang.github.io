以下是对斯坦福哲学百科“形而上学”词条2014年9月版本主体内容的翻译。于2021.12.31完成初稿，期间，与厦门大学前辈的译稿进行比对，于2022.1.23完成二稿。

有先前优秀的译稿在侧，岂敢不如履薄冰？但依旧先前译稿有诸多不足，如particular在同样的语义下，一会译为“殊相”一会译为“独立个体”，且有些许因语句复杂而存在明显的理解性错误。本稿尽可能避免这些瑕疵。同时，为了更好地阅读体验与能更清晰地比对，本文采取一句英文一句中译的呈现方式。

由于时间有限，且学识不足，疏漏、不当甚至错误之处在所难免，还望不吝赐教。

It is not easy to say what metaphysics is.

什么是形而上学，这谈起来并不容易。

Ancient and Medieval philosophers might have said that metaphysics was, like chemistry or astrology, to be defined by its subject-matter: metaphysics was the “science” that studied “being as such” or “the first causes of things” or “things that do not change”.

古代的和中世纪的哲学家们也许会说，形而上学是被它主题所限定的，诸如化学或是占星术：形而上学是一门“科学”，它研究的是“存在本身”、“事物的第一因”又或是“不变的事物”。

It is no longer possible to define metaphysics that way, for two reasons.

如今用之前的方法去定义形而上学不再可能，有以下两点原因。

First, a philosopher who denied the existence of those things that had once been seen as constituting the subject-matter of metaphysics—first causes or unchanging things—would now be considered to be making thereby a metaphysical assertion.

首先，一个哲学家如果否认那些曾经被视为构成形而上学主题事物——第一因或不变的事物——的存在，

那么他现在将被视为在做一种形而上学的断言。

Second, there are many philosophical problems that are now considered to be metaphysical problems (or at least partly metaphysical problems) that are in no way related to first causes or unchanging things—the problem of free will, for example, or the problem of the mental and the physical.

其次，许多现在被认为是形而上学问题（或至少在一定程度上是形而上学问题）的哲学问题，它们与第一因或不变的事物无关——例如，自由意志问题，或心理和物理的问题。

The first three sections of this entry examine a broad selection of problems considered to be metaphysical and discuss ways in which the purview of metaphysics has expanded over time.

本词条的前三节，将考察曾被视为形而上学的一系列问题，并讨论形而上学的范围是以哪些方式随着时间而扩展的。

We shall see that the central problems of metaphysics were significantly more unified in the Ancient and Medieval eras.

我们会发现，形而上学的核心问题在古代和中世纪明显更为统一。

Which raises a question—is there any common feature that unites the problems of contemporary metaphysics?

这就带来了一个问题：是否有什么共同特征可以将当代形而上学的问题联系在一起？

The final two sections discuss some recent theories of the nature and methodology of metaphysics. We will also consider arguments that metaphysics, however defined, is an impossible enterprise.

最后两节将讨论一些关于形而上学的本质及其方法论的近期理论。我们也将探讨此论点，即不论我们怎么去定义形而上学，都不可能尽善尽美。

The Word ‘Metaphysics’ and the Concept of Metaphysics
“形而上学”一词以及它的概念

The word ‘metaphysics’ is notoriously hard to define.

众所周知，“形而上学”这个词很难定义。

Twentieth-century coinages like ‘metalanguage’ and ‘metaphilosophy’ encourage the impression that metaphysics is a study that somehow “goes beyond” physics, a study devoted to matters that transcend the mundane concerns of Newton and Einstein and Heisenberg.

二十世纪的新词汇，如“元语言”、“元哲学”助长着这样一种关于形而上学的印象，即形而上学是一项以某种方式“超越”物理学的研究，一项致力于超越牛顿、爱因斯坦和海森堡对物质世界探索的研究。

This impression is mistaken.

这种印象是错误的。

The word ‘metaphysics’ is derived from a collective title of the fourteen books by Aristotle that we currently think of as making up Aristotle’s Metaphysics.

“形而上学”一词源于亚里士多德所著十四卷书的合集标题，我们目前认为这些书构成了亚里士多德的形而上学。

Aristotle himself did not know the word. (He had four names for the branch of philosophy that is the subject-matter of Metaphysics: ‘first philosophy’, ‘first science’, ‘wisdom’, and ‘theology’.)

亚里士多德本人并不知道这个词。（他为形而上学主题的哲学分支起了四个名字：“第一哲学”、“第一科学”、“智慧”和“神学”。）

At least one hundred years after Aristotle’s death, an editor of his works (in all probability, Andronicus of Rhodes) titled those fourteen books “Ta meta ta phusika”—“the after the physicals” or “the ones after the physical ones”—the “physical ones” being the books contained in what we now call Aristotle’s Physics.

亚里士多德死后至少一百年，他的一位著作编者（很可能是罗得岛的安德罗尼柯）将这十四本书命名为”Ta meta ta phusika”——“物理学之后”或“物理学诸卷之后的诸卷”——“物理学诸卷”是我们现在称之为亚里士多德的《物理学》中包含的书籍。

The title was probably meant to warn students of Aristotle’s philosophy that they should attempt Metaphysics only after they had mastered “the physical ones”, the books about nature or the natural world—that is to say, about change, for change is the defining feature of the natural world.

这个标题很可能是为了告诫亚里士多德哲学的学生，只有掌握了”物理学诸卷”，即关于自然或自然界的书籍——也就是说，关于变化的书籍，因为变化是自然界的定义特征——之后，他们才应该尝试形而上学。

This is the probable meaning of the title because Metaphysics is about things that do not change.

标题很可能就是这个含义，因为形而上学是关于不变的事物。

In one place, Aristotle identifies the subject-matter of first philosophy as “being as such”, and, in another as “first causes”.

在一处，亚里士多德将第一哲学的主题确定为“存在本身”，而在另一处则定为“第一因”。

It is a nice—and vexed—question what the connection between these two definitions is.

这是一个很好的也是恼人的问题，即这两个定义之间的联系是什么。

Perhaps this is the answer: The unchanging first causes have nothing but being in common with the mutable things they cause.

也许答案是这样：不变的第一因只与它们造成的可变事物有共通/共同之处。

Like us and the objects of our experience—they are, and there the resemblance ceases. (For a detailed and informative recent guide to Aristotle’s Metaphysics, see Politis 2004.)

就像我们和我们经验的对象一样——它们就是这样，并且相似就止于此。（有关亚里士多德形而上学的详细和翔实的最新指南，请参阅Politis 2004。）

Should we assume that ‘metaphysics’ is a name for that “science” which is the subject-matter of Aristotle’s Metaphysics?

我们是否应该假设“形而上学”是亚里士多德的形而上学主题的那门“科学”的一个名称？

If we assume this, we should be committed to something in the neighborhood of the following theses:

如果我们假设这一点，我们应赞同以下相近的假设：

The subject-matter of metaphysics is “being as such”

形而上学的主题是“存在本身”

The subject-matter of metaphysics is the first causes of things

形而上学的主题是事物的第一因

The subject-matter of metaphysics is that which does not change

形而上学的主题是不变的事物

Any of these three theses might have been regarded as a defensible statement of the subject-matter of what was called ‘metaphysics’ until the seventeenth century.

在十七世纪之前，这三个论点中的任何一个都可能被视为对所谓的“形而上学”主题的辩护。

But then, rather suddenly, many topics and problems that Aristotle and the Medievals would have classified as belonging to physics (the relation of mind and body, for example, or the freedom of the will, or personal identity across time) began to be reassigned to metaphysics.

但后来，相当突然地，亚里士多德和中世纪哲学家归类为属于物理学的许多主题和问题（例如，精神与肉体的关系，或自由意志，或人格同一性）开始被重新归于形而上学。

One might almost say that in the seventeenth century metaphysics began to be a catch-all category, a repository of philosophical problems that could not be otherwise classified as epistemology, logic, ethics or other branches of philosophy. (It was at about that time that the word ‘ontology’ was invented—to be a name for the science of being as such, an office that the word ‘metaphysics’ could no longer fill.)

人们几乎可以说，在十七世纪，形而上学开始成为一个包罗万象的范畴，一个哲学问题的宝库——包含那些不能被归类为认识论，逻辑学，伦理学或其他哲学分支的问题。（大约在那个时候，“本体论”这个词被发明出来了——作为存在本身科学的一个名称，成为一个“形而上学”这个词再也无法填补的领域。）

The academic rationalists of the post-Leibnizian school were aware that the word ‘metaphysics’ had come to be used in a more inclusive sense than it had once been.

后莱布尼茨学派的学术理性主义者意识到，“形而上学”这个词的使用比以前更具包容性。

Christian Wolff attempted to justify this more inclusive sense of the word by this device: while the subject-matter of metaphysics is being, being can be investigated either in general or in relation to objects in particular categories.

克里斯蒂安·沃尔夫（Christian Wolff）试图通过这种策略来证明这个词（形而上学）的这种更具包容性的含义：虽然形而上学的主题是存在的，但存在可以在一般情况下或与特定范畴中的对象相关进行研究。

He distinguished between ‘general metaphysics’ (or ontology), the study of being as such, and the various branches of ‘special metaphysics’, which study the being of objects of various special sorts, such as souls and material bodies. (He does not assign first causes to general metaphysics, however: the study of first causes belongs to natural theology, a branch of special metaphysics.)

他区分了“一般形而上学”（或本体论），即对存在本身的研究，以及“特殊形而上学”的各个分支，后者研究各种特殊对象的存在，如灵魂和肉体。（然而，他并没有将第一因归于一般的形而上学，对第一因的研究属于自然神学，这是特殊形而上学的一个分支。）

It is doubtful whether this maneuver is anything more than a verbal ploy.

令人怀疑的是，这种策略是否只是一种口头上的伎俩。

In what sense, for example, is the practitioner of rational psychology (the branch of special metaphysics devoted to the soul) engaged in a study of being?

例如，理性心理学（致力于灵魂的特殊形而上学的分支）的实践者在什么意义上从事对存在的研究？

Do souls have a different sort of being from that of other objects?—so that in studying the soul one learns not only about its nature (that is, its properties: rationality, immateriality, immortality, its capacity or lack thereof to affect the body …), but also about its “mode of being”, and hence learns something about being?

灵魂是否具有与其他对象不同的存在？这样，在研究灵魂时，人们不仅了解它的本质（即它的特性：理性，非物质性，不朽性，它影响身体的能力……），而且还了解它的“存在模式”，从而学习一些关于存在的东西？

It is certainly not true that all, or even very many, rational psychologists said anything, qua rational psychologists, that could plausibly be construed as a contribution to our understanding of being.

当然，并非所有、或是大多数的理性心理学家，以理性心理学家的身份，可以合理促进我们对存在的理解。

Perhaps the wider application of the word ‘metaphysics’ was due to the fact that the word ‘physics’ was coming to be a name for a new, quantitative science, the science that bears that name today, and was becoming increasingly inapplicable to the investigation of many traditional philosophical problems about changing things (and of some newly discovered problems about changing things).

也许“形而上学”这个词的更广泛应用是由于“物理学”这个词当时即将成为一种新的定量科学的名称，这个科学今天以这个名字命名，并且越来越不适用于研究许多关于变化事物的传统哲学问题（以及一些关于变化事物的新发现的问题）。

Whatever the reason for the change may have been, it would be flying in the face of current usage (and indeed of the usage of the last three or four hundred years) to stipulate that the subject-matter of metaphysics was to be the subject-matter of Aristotle’s Metaphysics.

无论这种变化的原因是什么，规定形而上学的主题是亚里士多德的形而上学的主题，这与当前的用法（实际上是过去三四百年的用法）背道而驰。

It would, moreover, fly in the face of the fact that there are and have been paradigmatic metaphysicians who deny that there are first causes—this denial is certainly a metaphysical thesis in the current sense—others who insist that everything changes (Heraclitus and any more recent philosopher who is both a materialist and a nominalist), and others still (Parmenides and Zeno) who deny that there is a special class of objects that do not change.

此外，它还将无视这样一个事实，即有并且一直有范式形而上学主义者否认有第一因——这种否认肯定是目前意义上的形而上学论点——其他人坚持认为一切都会改变（赫拉克利特和任何近来既是唯物主义者又是唯名主义者的哲学家们），还有一些人（巴门尼德和芝诺）否认有一类特殊的对象不会改变。

In trying to characterize metaphysics as a field, the best starting point is to consider the myriad topics traditionally assigned to it.

在试图将形而上学描述为一个领域时，最好的起点是考虑传统上归给它的种种主题。

The Problems of Metaphysics: the “Old” Metaphysics
形而上学的问题：“旧”形而上学


Being As Such, First Causes, Unchanging Things
存在本身，第一因，不变的事物

If metaphysics now considers a wider range of problems than those studied in Aristotle’s Metaphysics, those original problems continue to belong to its subject-matter.

如果说形而上学现在考虑的问题比亚里士多德的《形而上学》所研究的问题范围更广，那么这些原始问题仍然属于它的主题。

For instance, the topic of “being as such” (and “existence as such”, if existence is something other than being) is one of the matters that belong to metaphysics on any conception of metaphysics.

例如，“存在本身”（以及“作为存在本身的存在”，如果存在不是存在）的主题是任何形而上学概念中属于形而上学的问题之一。

The following theses are all paradigmatically metaphysical:

以下论点都是范式形而上学的：

“Being is; not-being is not” [Parmenides];

“存在者存在;不存在者不存在”[巴门尼德]；

“Essence precedes existence” [Avicenna, paraphrased];

“本质先于存在（实存）”[阿维森纳，转述];

“Existence in reality is greater than existence in the understanding alone” [St Anselm, paraphrased];

“仅存在于心灵中的观念不如既存在于心灵中又存在于现实中的观念完满”[圣·安塞尔谟，转述];

“Existence is a perfection” [Descartes, paraphrased];

“实存是一种完满”[笛卡尔，转述];

“Being is a logical, not a real predicate” [Kant, paraphrased];

“存在是一个逻辑的而非实在的谓词”[康德，转述];

“Being is the most barren and abstract of all categories” [Hegel, paraphrased];

“存在是所有范畴中最贫瘠、最抽象的”[黑格尔，转述];

“Affirmation of existence is in fact nothing but denial of the number zero” [Frege];

“对实存的肯定实际上只不过是对数字零的否定”[弗雷格];

“Universals do not exist but rather subsist or have being” [Russell, paraphrased];

“共相不存在，而是潜存或是拥有存在的可能”[罗素，转述];

“To be is to be the value of a bound variable” [Quine].

“去存在就是去成为一个约束变量”[蒯因]。

It seems reasonable, moreover, to say that investigations into non-being belong to the topic “being as such” and thus belong to metaphysics. (This did not seem reasonable to Meinong, who wished to confine the subject-matter of metaphysics to “the actual” and who therefore did not regard his Theory of Objects as a metaphysical theory. According to the conception of metaphysics adopted in this article, however, his thesis [paraphrased] “Predication is independent of being” is paradigmatically metaphysical.)

此外，似乎有理由说，对非存在的研究属于“存在本身”的主题，因而也属于形而上学。（这在迈农看来是不合理的，他希望将形而上学的主题限制在“现实”上，因此不认为他的《对象论》是形而上学的理论。然而，根据本文采用的形而上学概念，他的论点[转述]“主谓关系独立于存在”在范式上是形而上学的。

The topics “the first causes of things” and “unchanging things”—have continued to interest metaphysicians, though they are not now seen as having any important connection with the topic “being as such”.

“事物的第一因”和“不变的事物”这两个主题仍在引起形而上学家的兴趣，尽管现在看来，它们与“存在本身”这个话题没有那么重要的联系。

The first three of Aquinas’s Five Ways are metaphysical arguments on any conception of metaphysics.

阿奎那的“五路证明”的前三条，在任何形而上学概念中，都是形而上学的论证。

Additionally the thesis that there are no first causes and the thesis that there are no things that do not change count as metaphysical theses, for in the current conception of metaphysics, the denial of a metaphysical thesis is a metaphysical thesis.

此外，“不存在第一因”的论题、“不物不变”的论题都算作是形而上学的论题，因为在目前的形而上学概念中，否认某个形而上学的命题也是形而上学的论题。

No post-Medieval philosopher would say anything like this:

没有一个后中世纪的哲学家会这样说：

I study the first causes of things, and am therefore a metaphysician.

我研究事物的第一因，因此我是一个形而上学家。

My colleague Dr McZed denies that there are any first causes and is therefore not a metaphysician; she is rather, an anti-metaphysician.

我的同事McZed博士否认存在任何第一因，因此她不是形而上学家。相反，她是一个反形而上学的人。

In her view, metaphysics is a science with a non-existent subject-matter, like astrology.

在她看来，形而上学是一门不存在主题的科学，就像占星术一样。

This feature of the contemporary conception of metaphysics is nicely illustrated by a statement of Sartre’s:

萨特的论述很好地说明了当代形而上学概念的这一特征：

I do not think myself any less a metaphysician in denying the existence of God than Leibniz was in affirming it. (1949: 139)

我否定上帝的存在，而莱布尼茨肯定上帝的存在，但我不并认为我比他更不是一个形而上学家。(1949: 139)

An anti-metaphysician in the contemporary sense is not a philosopher who denies that there are objects of the sorts that an earlier philosopher might have said formed the subject-matter of metaphysics (first causes, things that do not change, universals, substances, …), but rather a philosopher who denies the legitimacy of the question whether there are objects of those sorts.

当代意义上的反形而上学主义者，不是一个否认「早期哲学家可能说过的」构成形而上学主题对象（第一因，不变的事物，共相，物质，……）存在的哲学家，而是一个否认“是否存在这些对象”这个问题合法性的哲学家。

The three original topics—the nature of being; the first causes of things; things that do not change—remained topics of investigation by metaphysicians after Aristotle.

三个原始主题——存在的本质；事物的第一因；不变的事物——仍然是亚里士多德之后形而上学家们研究的主题。

Another topic, discussed in the following subsection, occupies an intermediate position between Aristotle and his successors.

在下一小节中讨论的另一个主题在亚里士多德和他的后继者们之间占据中间位置。

Categories of Being and Universals
存在的范畴和共相

We human beings sort things into various classes. And we often suppose that the classes into which we sort things enjoy a kind of internal unity.

我们人类将事物分为不同的类别。我们经常假设，我们把事物分到的各种类别中，有一种内在的统一。

In this respect they differ from sets in the strict sense of the word. (And no doubt in others. It would seem, for example, that we think of the classes we sort things into—biological species, say—as comprising different members at different times.)

在这方面，它们与严格字面意义上的集合不同。（毫无疑问，在其他方面上也不同。例如，我们似乎认为我们分到的类—例如生物物种—在不同时期由不同的成员组成。

The classes into which we sort things are in most cases “natural” classes, classes whose membership is in some important sense uniform—“kinds”. We shall not attempt an account or definition of ‘natural class’ here.

在大多数情况下，我们将事物分到的类是“自然”类，其成员在某种重要意义上是统一的类——“种类”。在这里，我们不试图解释或定义“自然类”。

Examples must suffice. There are certainly sets whose members do not make up natural classes: a set that contains all dogs but one, and a set that contains all dogs and exactly one cat do not correspond to natural classes in anyone’s view.

举例就足够了。当然，有些集合的成员不构成自然类：一个包含除一只狗之外的所有狗的集合，以及一个包含所有狗和恰好一只猫的集合，在任何人看来都不符合自然类。

And it is tempting to suppose that there is a sense of “natural” in which dogs make up a natural class, to suppose that in dividing the world into dogs and non-dogs, we “cut nature at the joints”.

人们很容易假设狗构成的一个自然类中存在“自然”的意义，也很容易假设将世界划分为狗和非狗时，我们“在关节处切割了自然”。

It is, however, a respectable philosophical thesis that the idea of a natural class cannot survive philosophical scrutiny.

然而，“自然类的概念不能经受哲学审查”是一个值得尊敬的哲学论点。

If that respectable thesis is true, the topic “the categories of being” is a pseudo-topic.

如果这个值得尊敬的论点是正确的，那么“存在的范畴”就是一个伪话题。

Let us simply assume that the respectable thesis is false and that things fall into various natural classes—hereinafter, simply classes.

让我们简单地假设，这个值得尊敬的论点是错误的，即事物属于各种自然类——以下简称为类。

Some of the classes into which we sort things are more comprehensive than others: all dogs are animals, but not all animals are dogs; all animals are living organisms, but not all living organisms are animals ….

我们分的一些类比其他类更全面：所有的狗都是动物，但并非所有动物都是狗;所有的动物都是活生物体，但并非所有的活生物体都是动物……

Now the very expression “sort things into classes” suggests that there is a most comprehensive class: the class of things, the class of things that can be sorted into classes.

现在，“将事物分为类”这个表达本身就表明存在一个最全面的类：事物类，可以被分成类的事物的类。

But is this so?—and if it is so, are there classes that are “just less comprehensive” than this universal class?

但事实果真如此吗？如果是这样，有没有一些类比这个共相类“略微不全面一点”呢？

If there are, can we identify them?—and are there a vast (perhaps even an infinite) number of them, or some largish, messy number like forty-nine, or some small, neat number like seven or four?

如果有的话，我们能辨别出它们吗？——它们的数量是巨大的（甚至可能是无限的），还是像49这样庞大而凌乱的数字，或者像7或4这样小而整齐的数字？

Let us call any such less comprehensive classes the ‘categories of being’ or the ‘ontological categories’. (The former term, if not the latter, presupposes a particular position on one question about the nature of being: that everything is, that the universal class is the class of beings, the class of things that are. It thus presupposes that Meinong was wrong to say that “there are things of which it is true that there are no such things”.)

让我们把任何这种不那么全面的类称为“存在的范畴”或“本体论范畴”。（前一个术语，如果不是后者的话，就在关于存在本质的问题上预设了一个特定的立场：一切都存在，普遍类是存在物的类，是所是者的类。因此，它预先假定迈农说“存在那些不存在的事物”是错误的。）

The topic “the categories of being” is intermediate between the topic “the nature of being” and the topics that fall under the post-Medieval conception of metaphysics for a reason that can be illustrated by considering the problem of universals.

“存在的范畴”这一主题介于“存在的本质”和归入后中世纪形而上学概念的主题之间，其原因可以通过考虑共相问题来说明。

Universals, if they indeed exist, are, in the first instance, properties or qualities or attributes (i.e., “ductility” or “whiteness”) that are supposedly universally “present in” the members of classes of things and relations (i.e., “being to the north of”) that are supposedly universally present in the members of classes of sequences of things. “In the first instance”: it may be that things other than qualities and relations are universals, although qualities and relations are the items most commonly put forward as examples of universals.

共相，如果它们确实存在，首要而言是一些特性、品质或属性，这些据称普遍“存在于”事物类与关系类中，而关系本身（如，在北方）则普遍地出现在事物的序列对组成的类别中。“首要而言”：除了最常被作为共相提及的品质和关系外，可能还有其他事物是共相。

It may be that the novel War and Peace is a universal, a thing that is in some mode present in each of the many tangible copies of the novel.

也许小说《战争与和平》是一个共相，它以某种方式存在于这部小说的许多有形书册中。

It may be that the word “horse” is a universal, a thing that is present in each of the many audible utterances of the word.

也许“马”这个词是一个共相，它存在于这个词许多可听见的话语中。

And it may be that natural classes or kinds are themselves universals—it may be that there is such a thing as “the horse” or the species Equus caballus, distinct from its defining attribute “being a horse” or “equinity”, and in some sense “present in” each horse. (Perhaps some difference between the attribute “being a horse” and the attribute “being either a horse or a kitten” explains why the former is the defining attribute of a kind and the latter is not. Perhaps the former attribute exists and the latter does not; perhaps the former has the second-order attribute “naturalness” and the latter does not; perhaps the former is more easily apprehended by the intellect than the latter.)

也许自然类或种类本身就是共相——或许存在着与“是一匹马”、“马性”这种定义性属性不同的另一种共相，即出现于每一匹马中的“马”或“野马属”物种这个类。（也许属性“是一匹马”和属性“是一匹马或一只小猫”之间的一些差异，解释了为什么前者是某种类的定义属性，而后者不是。也许前者存在，而后者不存在;也许前者具有“自然性”这种二阶属性，而后者则没有;也许前者比后者更容易被思维所理解。）

The thesis that universals exist—or at any rate “subsist” or “have being”—is variously called ‘realism’ or ‘Platonic realism’ or ‘platonism’.

共相存在这一论题——或其至少“潜存”或“有是”——被称为“实在论”或“柏拉图实在论”或“柏拉图主义”。

All three terms are objectionable. Aristotle believed in the reality of universals, but it would be at best an oxymoron to call him a platonist or a Platonic realist.

这三个术语都是有争议的。亚里士多德相信共相的真实性，但称他为柏拉图主义者或柏拉图理念论者，无论如何都是矛盾的。

And ‘realism’ tout court has served as a name for a variety of philosophical theses.

“实在论”只不过是一个涵盖形形色色哲学论题的名称罢了。

The thesis that universals do not exist—do not so much as subsist; have no being of any sort—is generally called ‘nominalism’.

共相不存在——甚至不是潜存；没有任何形式的存在——通常被称为“唯名论”。

This term, too is objectionable. At one time, those who denied the existence of universals were fond of saying things like:

这个词也是有争议的。一度，那些否认共相存在的人喜欢说这样的话：

There is no such thing as “being a horse”: there is only the name [nomen, gen. nominis] “horse”, a mere flatus vocis [puff of sound].

没有“是一匹马”这样的东西：只有[nomen，gen. nominis]“马”这个名称，它仅仅是一阵flatus vocis[一阵声音]。

Present-day nominalists, however, are aware, if earlier nominalists were not, that if the phrase ‘the name “horse” ’ designated an object, the object it designated would itself be a universal or something very like one.

然而，当今的唯名论者察觉到——如果早期的唯名论者尚未意识到——如果短语“‘马’这个名称”指向了一个对象，那么它指定的对象本身就是一个共相或与其非常相似的东西。

It would not be a mere puff of sound but would rather be what was common to the many puffs of sound that were its tokens.

它不会仅仅是一阵声音，而是许多声音的共同点，这些声音是它的标志。

The old debate between the nominalists and the realists continues to the present day.

唯名论者和唯实论者之间的古老论辩一直持续到今天。

Most realists suppose that universals constitute one of the categories of being.

大多数实在论者认为共相构成了存在的一个范畴。

This supposition could certainly be disputed without absurdity.

这种假设受到质疑十分合理。

Perhaps there is a natural class of things to which all universals belong but which contains other things as well (and is not the class of all things).

也许有这样一个自然类，它包含所有共相，但也包含着其他事物（而又不是万物的类）。

Perhaps, for example, numbers and propositions are not universals, and perhaps numbers and propositions and universals are all members of a class of “abstract objects”, a class that some things do not belong to.

也许，例如，数字和命题不是共相，也许数字、命题和共相都是一类“抽象对象”的成员，这“抽象对象”的类不包含某些事物。

Or perhaps there is such a thing as “the whiteness of the Taj Mahal” and perhaps this object and the universal “whiteness”—but not the Taj Mahal itself—both belong to the class of “properties”.

或者也许有“泰姬陵的白”这样的事物，也许这个对象和共相的“白”——但不是泰姬陵本身——都属于“属性”这一类。

Let us call such a class—a proper subclass of an ontological category, a natural class that is neither the class of all things nor one of the ontological categories—an ‘ontological sub-category’.

让我们称这样一个类为——一个本体论范畴的适当子类，一个「既不是万物的类、也不是本体论范畴之一的」自然类——一个“本体论子范畴”。

It may indeed be that universals make up a sub-category of being and are members of the category of being “abstract object”.

也许共相确实构成了存在的一个子范畴，并且是“抽象对象”范畴的成员。

But few if any philosophers would suppose that universals were members of forty-nine sub-categories—much less of a vast number or an infinity of sub-categories.

但是，很少有哲学家会认为共相是49个子范畴的成员之一，更不用说大量或无限的子范畴了。

Most philosophers who believe in the reality of universals would want to say that universals, if they do not constitute an ontological category, at least constitute one of the “higher” sub-categories.

大多数相信共相实在性的哲学家会想说，如果共相不构成一个本体论范畴，也至少构成“较高”的子范畴之一。

If dogs form a natural class, this class is—by the terms of our definition—an ontological sub-category.

如果狗构成了一个自然类，那么根据我们的定义，这个类就是一个本体论的子范畴。

And this class will no doubt be a subclass of many sub-categories: the genus canis, the class (in the biological sense) mammalia, …, and so through a chain of sub-categories that eventually reaches some very general sub-category like “substance” or “material object”.

毫无疑问，这个类将是许多子范畴的一个亚类：犬属，哺乳纲（在生物学意义上）等等，也因此通过一系列子范畴，最终到达一些非常一般的子范畴，如“实体”或“物质对象”。

Thus, although dogs may compose an ontological sub-category, this sub-category—unlike the category “universal”—is one of the “lower” ones.

因此，尽管狗可以构成一个本体论的子范畴，但这一子范畴不同于“共相”范畴，是“较低”的子范畴之一。

These reflections suggest that the topic “the categories of being” should be understood to comprehend both the categories of being sensu stricto and their immediate sub-categories.

这些思考表明，“存在的范畴”这一主题应被理解为，既包含严格意义上的范畴，也包含其紧接的子范畴。

Does the topic “the categories of being” belong to metaphysics in the “old” sense?

“存在的范畴”这个话题是否属于“旧”意义上的形而上学？

A case can be made for saying that it does, based on the fact that Plato’s theory of forms (universals, attributes) is a recurrent theme in Aristotle’s Metaphysics.

基于柏拉图的相论（共相，属性）是亚里士多德《形而上学》中反复出现的主题，可以说它确实如此。

In Metaphysics, two of Plato’s central theses about the forms come in for vigorous criticism:

在《形而上学》中，柏拉图关于相的两个核心论点遭到了有力的批评：

(i)that things that would, if they existed, be “inactive” (the forms) could be the primary beings, the “most real” things, and (ii) that the attributes of things exist “separately” from the things whose attributes they are.

（i）如果存在的话，“不活跃”的事物（形式）可能是首要的存在，是“最真实”的事物；（ii）事物的属性与它们的属性“分开”存在。

We shall be concerned only with (ii).

我们将只考虑（ii）。

In the terminology of the Schools, that criticism can be put this way: Plato wrongly believed that universals existed ante res (prior to objects); the correct view is that universals exist in rebus (in objects).

用学院派的术语，这种批评可以这样说：柏拉图错误地认为共相在对象前（先于对象）就已经存在了，而正确的观点是共相存在于对象中（在对象中）。

It is because this aspect of the problem of universals—whether universals exist ante res or in rebus—is discussed at length in Metaphysics, that a strong case can be made for saying that the problem of universals falls under the old conception of metaphysics. (And the question whether universals, given that they exist at all, exist ante res or in rebus is as controversial in the twenty-first century as it was in the thirteenth century and the fourth century B.C.E.)

正是因为共相问题的这一方面——无论共相是在对象前还是在对象中存在——在《形而上学》中得到了详尽的讨论，所以可以有力地说，共相问题属于旧的形而上学概念。（鉴于共相的存在，“共相先于对象还是在对象中存在”这个问题在21世纪、与在13世纪或在公元前4世纪一样是具有争议的）。

If we do decide that the problem of universals belongs to metaphysics on the old conception, then, since we have liberalized the old conception by applying to it the contemporary rule that the denial of a metaphysical position is to be regarded as a metaphysical position, we shall have to say that the question whether universals exist at all is a metaphysical question under the old conception—and that nominalism is therefore a metaphysical thesis.

如果我们真的认为共相问题属于旧概念上的形而上学，那么，因为我们放宽了旧概念（对形而上学的限制）而采取当代的规定，「即否定形而上学的立场也应被视为形而上学的立场」。我们就不得不说，在旧概念下，“共相到底存在与否”这个问题是形而上学的问题，因此唯名论是形而上学的论题。

There is, however, also a case to made against classifying the problem of universals as a problem of metaphysics in the (liberalized) old sense.

然而，也有理由反对将共相问题归类为（放宽限制的）旧意义上的形而上学问题。

For there is more to the problem of universals than the question whether universals exist and the question whether, if they do exist, their existence is ante res or in rebus.

因为共相问题不仅仅是「共相是否存在的问题」，也不仅仅是「如果它们确实存在，是先于对象还是在对象中存在的问题」。

For example, the problem of universals also includes questions about the relation between universals (if such there be) and the things that are not universals, the things usually called particulars.

例如，共相问题还包括关于共相（如果存在）与非共相（通常称为殊相）之间的关系问题。

Aristotle did not consider these questions in the Metaphysics.

亚里士多德在《形而上学》中没有考虑这些问题。

One might therefore plausibly contend that only one part of the problem of universals (the part that pertains to the existence and nature of universals) belongs to metaphysics in the old sense.

因此，人们可能会合理地争辩，只有一部分的共相问题（与共相的存在和性质有关的那部分）属于旧意义上的形而上学。

At one time, a philosopher might have said,The universal “doghood” is a thing that does not change.

曾经，一位哲学家可能会说，“狗性”这一共相是不变的。

Therefore, questions about its nature belong to metaphysics, the science of things that do not change.

因此，关于其本质的问题属于形而上学，即不变事物的科学。

But dogs are things that change. Therefore, questions concerning the relation of dogs to doghood do not belong to metaphysics.

但狗是会改变的事物。因此，「狗与狗性」关系的问题不属于形而上学。

But no contemporary philosopher would divide the topics that way—not even if he or she believed that doghood existed and was a thing that did not change.

但是，没有一个当代哲学家会以这种方式划分主题，即使他或她相信“狗性”存在，且是一种不会改变的东西。

A contemporary philosopher—if that philosopher concedes that there is any problem that can properly be called “the problem of universals”—will see the problem of universals as a problem properly so called, as a problem having the kind of internal unity that leads philosophers to speak of a philosophical problem.

一个当代哲学家——如果他（她）承认「存在任何可以真正被称为“共相问题”的问题」——将共相问题视为一个名副其实的问题，即一个具有某种内在统一性，使其被哲学家称为哲学问题的问题。

And the same point applies to the topic “the categories of being”: every philosopher who is willing to say that “What are the categories of being?” is a meaningful question will assign every aspect of that question to metaphysics.

这一点也适用于“存在的范畴”这个话题：每个愿意说“存在的范畴是什么？”是一个有意义的问题的哲学家，都会把这个问题的各个方面都归入形而上学。

Let us consider some aspects of the problem of universals that concern changing things. (That is, that concern particulars—for even if there are particulars that do not change, most of the particulars that figure in discussions of the problem of universals as examples are things that change.)

让我们考虑一下共相问题中关于变化事物的的某些方面。（也就是说，这涉及殊相。因为虽然存在不变的殊相，但是在共相问题的讨论中，作为例子出现的殊相大都是可变事物。）

Consider two white particulars—the Taj Mahal, say, and the Washington Monument.

考虑两个白色的殊相——比如泰姬陵和华盛顿纪念碑。

And suppose that both these particulars are white in virtue of (i.e., their being white consists in) their bearing some one, identifiable relation to the universal “whiteness”.

假设这两个殊相都是白色的是在于它们所承载的某种关系，即它们与共相“白色性”的可识别关系。

Suppose further that we are able to single out this relation by some sort of act of intellectual attention or abstraction, and that (having done so) we have given it the name “falling under”.

进一步假设，我们能够通过某种智力关注或抽象活动来挑出这种关系，并且（在这样做之后）我们给它起了个名字“归入”。

All white things and only white things fall under whiteness, and falling under whiteness is what it is to be white. (We pass over many questions that would have to be addressed if we were discussing the problem of universals for its own sake. For example, both blueness and redness are spectral color-properties, and whiteness is not. Does this fact imply that “being a spectral color-property” is, as one might say, a second-order universal? If so, does blueness “fall under” this universal in the same sense as the sense in which a copy of Philosophical Studies falls under blueness?)

所有且仅有白色的东西归入白色性，而归入白色性的就是白的。（我们忽略了许多问题，如果我们就共相问题本身来讨论，就必须解决这些它们。例如，蓝色性和红色性都是光谱颜色特性，而白色性则不是。这一事实是否意味着“作为光谱颜色特性”，人们或许会说，是一个二阶共相？如果是，那么「蓝色性“归入”这种共相」，与「一册印刷的《哲学研究》“归入”蓝色性」是同一个意思吗？

Now what can we say about this relation, this “falling under”?

现在我们能对这种“归入”关系说些什么呢？

What is it about the two objects whiteness and the Taj Mahal that is responsible for the fact that the latter falls under the former?

关于白色性和泰姬陵这两个对象，究竟是什么导致了后者归入前者？

Is the Taj perhaps a “bundle” of universalia ante res, and does it fall under whiteness in virtue of the fact that whiteness is one of the universals that is a constituent of the bundle that it is?

泰姬陵是一“束”先于对象存在的共相吗？它是否由于白色性是构成这束的共相成分之一这一事实，而归入白色性？

Or might it be that a particular like the Taj, although it indeed has universals as constituents, is something more than its universal constituents?

或者，是否像泰姬陵这样的殊相，虽然它确实由共相所组成，但它还包含有共相之外的成分。

Might it be that the Taj has a constituent that is not a universal, a “substrate”, a particular that is in some sense property-less and that holds the universal constituents of the Taj together—that “bundles” them? (If we take that position, then we may want to say, with Armstrong (1989: 94–96), that the Taj is a ‘thick particular’ and its substrate a ‘thin particular’: a thick particular being a thin particular taken together with the properties it bundles.)

难道泰姬陵有一个成分，它不是一个共相而是一个“基质”，一个在某种意义上无特性的殊相，并且将泰姬陵的共相成分结合在一起，即将它们“束”在一起？（如果我们持这种立场，那么我们可能会同阿姆斯特朗（Armstrong，1989：94-96）那样，认为泰姬陵是一个“厚的殊相”，它的基质是一个“薄的殊相”，厚的殊相是由一个薄的殊相与它“束”着的特性合在一起的。

Or might the Taj have constituents that are neither universals nor substrates?

或者泰姬陵是否有既不是共相又不是基质的成分？

Might we have been too hasty when we defined ‘particulars’ as things that are not universals?

当我们将“殊相”定义为「不是共相」时，是否太草率了？

Could there perhaps be two kinds of non-universals, concrete non-universals or concrete individuals (those would be the particulars, thick or thin), and abstract non-universals or abstract individuals (‘accidents’ or ‘tropes’ or ‘property instances’), things that are properties or qualities (and relations as well), things like “the (individual) whiteness of the Taj Mahal”?

也许会有两种非共相，「具体的非共相或具体的个体（它们是厚或薄的殊相），抽象的非共相或抽象的个体（“偶性”或“特普”或“特性实例”）」，它们是属性或品质（以及关系），如“泰姬陵（个体）的白色性”？

Is the Taj perhaps a bundle not of universals but of accidents?

或许泰姬陵并不是由一“束”共相而是一束偶性？

Or is it composed of a substrate and a bundle of accidents?

又或是由一个基质和一束偶性构成？

And we cannot neglect the possibility that Aristotle was right and that universals exist only in rebus.

我们无法忽略这样一种可能，即亚里士多德是对的，共相仅存在于对象中。

If that is so, we must ask what the relation is between the matter that composes a particular and the universals that inhere in it—that inhere simultaneously in “this” matter and in “that” matter.

若确实如此，我们就必须得追问，构成一个殊相的质料与它内含的共相（这共相同时存在于“这个”和“那个”质料）有什么关系？

The series of questions that was set out in the preceding paragraph was introduced by observing that the problem of universals includes both questions about the existence and nature of universals and questions about how universals are related to the particulars that fall under them.

在上一段的一系列问题中，我们注意到，共相问题既包括关于「共相的存在和性质」的问题，也包括关于「共相如何与归入它们的殊相相关联」的问题。

Many of the theories that were alluded to in that series of questions could be described as theories of the “ontological structure” of non-universals.

在这一系列问题中提到的许多理论，都可以被描述为非共相的“本体论（存在论）结构”的理论。

We can contrast ontological structure with mereological structure.

我们可以将本体论结构与部分论结构进行对比。

A philosophical question concerns the mereological structure of an object if it is a question about the relation between that object and those of its constituents that belong to the same ontological category as the object.

如果一个哲学问题，它是一个关于「该对象与其（同属一个本体论范畴的）构成物之间的关系」的问题，那么它就是一个部分论结构的问题。

For example, the philosopher who asks whether the Taj Mahal has a certain block of marble among its constituents essentially or only accidentally is asking a question about the mereological structure of the Taj, since the block and the building belong to the same ontological category.

例如，当哲学家问，泰姬陵的构成物中有一块大理石，这是本质上地还是仅仅是偶然地呢？这就相当于在问一个关于泰姬陵分体论结构的问题，因为那石块和建筑物属于同一个本体论范畴。

But the philosopher who asks whether the Taj has “whiteness” as a constituent and the philosopher who supposes that the Taj does have this property-constituent and asks, “What is the nature of this relation ‘constituent of’ that ‘whiteness’ bears to the Taj?” are asking questions about its ontological structure.

但是，如果哲学家问泰姬陵是否以“白色性”为构成物，如果又有假设「泰姬陵确实具有这种特性构成物」的哲学家问：“‘白色性’是泰姬陵的‘构成物’这一关系的本质是什么？”，他们都是在追问其本体论结构。

Many philosophers have supposed that particulars fall under universals by somehow incorporating them into their ontological structure.

许多哲学家认为，殊相以某种方式将共相纳入其本体论结构，因此殊相归入共相。

And other philosophers have supposed that the ontological structure of a particular incorporates individual properties or accidents—and that an accident is an accident of a certain particular just in virtue of being a constituent of that particular.

还有一部分哲学家认为，一个殊相的本体论结构包含了个体特性或偶性——而偶性是某个殊相的偶性，仅仅因为它是该殊相的构成物。

Advocates of the existence of ante res universals, and particularly those who deny that these universals are constituents of particulars, tend to suppose that universals abound—that there is not only such a universal as whiteness but such a universal as “being both white and round and either shiny or not made of silver”.

主张共相先于对象存在的人，特别是那些否认共相是殊相构成物的人，都倾向于认为共相比比皆是——不仅有诸如白色性的共相，而且有“既白又圆，要么有光泽的、要么不是银制的”这样的共相。

Advocates of other theories of universals are almost always less liberal in the range of universals whose existence they will allow.

其他共相理论的拥护者几乎都支持，「存在的共相」的范围并不那么宽泛。

The advocate of in rebus universals is unlikely to grant the existence of “being both white and round and either shiny or not made of silver”, even in the case in which there is an object that is both white and round and either shiny or not made of silver (such as a non-shiny white plastic ball).

主张共相存在于对象中的人，不大可能承认“既白又圆、要么有光泽的，要么不是银制的”的存在，即使存在一个既白又圆、要么有光泽，要么由银制成的对象（例如不闪亮的白色塑料球）。

The two topics “the categories of being” and “the ontological structure of objects” are intimately related to each other and to the problem of universals.

“存在的范畴”和“对象的本体论结构”这两个主题彼此密切相关，也与共相问题密切相关。

It is not possible to propose a solution to the problem of universals that does not have implications for the topic “the categories of being”. (Even nominalism implies that at least one popular candidate for the office “ontological category” is non-existent or empty.)

不可能给出一个不牵涉“存在的范畴”这一主题却能解决共相问题的办法。（甚至唯名论也意味着至少一种流行的“本体论范畴”（即共相）其成员不存在或是空集。）

It is certainly possible to maintain that there are ontological categories that are not directly related to the problem of universals (“proposition”, “state of affairs”, “event”, “mere possibile”), but any philosopher who maintains this will nevertheless maintain that if there are universals they make up at least one of the higher ontological sub-categories.

当然可以坚持认为，有些本体论范畴与共相问题没有直接关系（“命题”、“事态”、“事件”、“纯粹的可能存在物”），但任何坚持这一点的哲学家仍会坚持认为，如果存在共相，它们至少构成一个较高的本体论子范畴。

And it seems that it is possible to speak of ontological structure only if one supposes that there are objects of different ontological categories.

似乎只有当人们假设「存在不同本体论范畴的对象」时，才有可能谈论本体论结构。

So whatever metaphysics comprehends, it must comprehend every aspect of the problem of universals and every aspect of the topics “the categories of being” and “the ontological structure of objects”.

因此，无论形而上学包含什么，它都必须包含共相问题的每一方面，以及“存在的范畴”和“对象的本体论结构”这两个主题的每一方面。

For a recent investigation of the problems that have been discussed in this section, see Lowe (2006).

有关本节中讨论到的问题的最新研究，请参阅Lowe （2006）。

We turn now to a topic that strictly speaking belongs to “the categories of being”, but which is important enough to be treated separately.

现在，我们转向一个严格说来属于“存在的范畴”的话题，该话题足够重要，值得单独讨论。

Substance
物质

Some things (if they exist at all) are present only “in” other things: a smile, a haircut (product, not process), a hole ….

有些东西（如果它们的确存在）只出现于其他事物中：一个微笑，一个发型（指结果，而非过程），一个洞….

Such things may be opposed to things that exist “in their own right”.

这些东西可能与“依其本身”存在的东西相对。

Metaphysicians call the things that exist in their own right ‘substances’. Aristotle called them ‘protai ousiai’ or “primary beings”.

形而上学家称「依其自身存在」的事物为“实体”。亚里士多德称他们为“第一实体”（protai ousiai）。

They make up the most important of his ontological categories.

它们构成了他的本体论范畴中最重要的部分。

Several features define protai ousiai: they are subjects of predication that cannot themselves be predicated of things (they are not universals); things exist “in” them, but they do not exist “in” things (they are not accidents like Socrates’ wisdom or his ironic smile); they have determinate identities (essences).

有几个特征明确了“第一实体”：它们是谓词的主词且本身不是谓词（它们不是共相）;事物存在于它们“之中”，但它们并不存在于事物“之中”（它们不是像「苏格拉底的智慧或他反讽的微笑」那样的偶性）;它们具有明确的特同一性（本质）。

This last feature could be put this way in contemporary terms: if the prote ousia x exists at a certain time and the prote ousia y exists at some other time, it makes sense to ask whether x and y are the same, are numerically identical (and the question must have a determinate answer); and the question whether a given prote ousia would exist in some set of counterfactual circumstances must likewise have an answer (at least if the circumstances are sufficiently determinate—if, for example, they constitute a possible world. More on this in the next section).

最后一个特征可以用当代术语来阐释：如果第一实体x在某个时间存在，且第一实体 y在另一个时间存在，那么问x和y是否相同、在数值上是否相同（并且问题必须有一个确定的答案）是有意义的;而一个特定的“第一实体”在某些反事实的情况下是否存在，这一问题也必须有一个回答（至少如果这些情况充分确定——例如，如果它们构成了一个可能世界。在下一节中将对此进行详细介绍）。

It is difficult to suppose that smiles or holes have this sort of determinate identity.

很难假设微笑或洞具有这种确定性的特性。

To ask whether the smile Socrates smiled today is the smile he smiled yesterday (or is the smile he would have smiled if Crito had asked one of his charmingly naïve questions) can only be a question about descriptive identity.

要问苏格拉底今天的微笑是否是他昨天的微笑（或者当克里托问了一个迷人而天真的问题时，他露出的微笑），这只是一个关于「描述性同一」的问题。

Aristotle uses ‘(prote) ousia’ not only as a count-noun but as a mass term. (He generally writes ‘ousia’ without qualification when he believes that the context will make it clear that he means ‘prote ousia’.)

亚里士多德不仅把“（第一）实体”当作一个可数名词，而且作为一个集合术语。（当他认为上下文意义明确他指的是“第一实体”时，通常不加限定地写“实体”。）

For example, he not only asks questions like “Is Socrates a (prote) ousia?” and “What is a (prote) ousia”?, but questions like “What is the (prote) ousia of Socrates?” and “What is (prote) ousia?” (Which question he is asking sometimes has to be inferred from the context, since there is no indefinite article in Greek.)

例如，他不仅问“苏格拉底是（第一）实体吗？”、“什么是一个（第一）实体？”，而且还会问“什么是苏格拉底的（第一）实体？”、“什么是（第一）实体？”这样的问题。（由于希腊语中没有不定冠词，他所问的究竟是哪一个问题，有时必须要根据上下文语境推断。）

In the count-noun sense of the term, Aristotle identifies at least some (protai) ousiai with ta hupokeimena or “underlying things”.

在这个词的可数名词意义上，亚里士多德将至少一些（第一）实体等同于基质。

Socrates, for example, is a hupokeimenon in that he “lies under” the in rebus universals under which he falls and the accidents that inhere in him. ‘To hupokeimenon’ has an approximate Latin equivalent in ‘substantia’, “that which stands under”. (Apparently, “to stand under” and “to lie under” are equally good metaphorical descriptions of the relations a thing bears to its qualities and accidents.)

例如，苏格拉底就是一个基质，因为他“躺在”他所归入的「在对象中存在的」共相下面，还“躺在”他固有的偶性下面。 “To hupokeimenon （躺在下面）”有一个几乎同义的拉丁词语，即“substantia（站在下面）”。（显然，“站在下面”和“躺在下面”是对「事物与其品质和偶性之间关系」的很好的隐喻性描述。）

Owing both to the close association of (protai) ousiai and hupokeimena in Aristotle’s philosophy and to the absence of a suitable Latin equivalent of ‘ousia’ ‘substantia’ became the customary Latin translation of the count-noun ‘(prote) ousia’.

由于亚里士多德哲学中，（第一）实体和基质在的紧密联系，以及拉丁语中缺乏合适的词来对应“ousia”，“substantia”就成了可数名词‘(prote) ousia’的惯常拉丁翻译。

The question whether there in fact are substances continues to be one of the central questions of metaphysics. Several closely related questions are: How, precisely, should the concept of substance be understood?;

「实体是否真的存在」仍然是形而上学的核心问题之一。几个密切相关的问题是：确切地说，究竟应如何理解实体概念？

Which of the items (if any of them) among those we encounter in everyday life are substances?;

我们在日常生活中遇到的物品，哪些（如果有的话）是实体？

If there are substances at all, how many of them are there?—is there only one as Spinoza contended, or are there many as most of the rationalists supposed?; What kinds of substances are there?—are there immaterial substances, eternal substances, necessarily existent substances?

如果确有实体，它们有多少呢？——是像斯宾诺莎所主张的只有一种，还是像大多数唯理论者所认为的有那么多？;有哪些种类的实体？——有非实质的实体、永恒的实体、必然存在的实体吗？

It must be emphasized that there is no universally accepted and precise definition of ‘substance’.

必须强调的是，目前普遍接受的、确切的“实体”定义依旧难觅。

Depending on how one understood the word (or the concept) one might say either that Hume denied that there were any substances or that he held that the only substances (or the only substances of which we have any knowledge) were impressions and ideas.

取决于一个人如何理解这个词（或概念），他可能会说休谟否认存在任何实体，或者他会认为仅有的实体（或者我们唯一能知晓的实体）是印象和观念。

It would seem, however, that most philosophers who are willing to use the word ‘substance’ at all would deny that any of the following (if they exist) are substances:

然而似乎，大多数愿意使用“实体”这个词的哲学家，都会否认以下任何一种（如果存在的话）是实体：

· Universals and other abstract objects. (It should be noted that Aristotle criticized Plato for supposing that the protai ousiai were ante res universals.)

共相和其他抽象对象。（应该指出的是，亚里士多德批评柏拉图所假设的「第一实体是先于对象存在的共相」。）

· Events, processes, or changes. (But some metaphysicians contend that substance/event is a false dichotomy.)

事件、过程或变化。（不过有些形而上学主义者认为实体/事件是一种错误的二分法。）

· Stuffs, such as flesh or iron or butter. (Unfortunately for beginning students of metaphysics, the usual meaning of ‘substance’ outside philosophy is stuff. Aristotle criticized “the natural philosophers” for supposing that the prote ousia could be a stuff—water or air or fire or matter.)

物质（质料？），如肉、铁或黄油。（不幸的是，对于形而上学的初学者来说，“实体”一词在哲学之外的含义通常是物质（质料？）。亚里士多德批评“自然哲学家们”认为第一实体可能是一种物质（质料？），如水、空气、火等质料。

The nature of being, the problem of universals, and the nature of substance have been recognized as topics that belong to “metaphysics” by almost everyone who has used the word. We now turn to topics that belong to metaphysics only in the post-Medieval sense.

「存在的本质、共相问题、以及实体的本质」，几乎被每个用过“形而上学”这个词的人认为属于“形而上学”的问题。我们现在转向只属于后中世纪意义上的形而上学的主题。

The Problems of Metaphysics: the “New” Metaphysics
形而上学的问题：“新”形而上学

Modality
模态

Philosophers have long recognized that there is an important distinction within the class of true propositions: the distinction between those propositions that might have been false and those that could not have been false (those that must be true).

哲学家们早就注意到，在真命题的类中有一个重要的区分：「本可能为假」的命题和「不可能为假」的命题（即必然为真）。

Compare, for example, the proposition that Paris is the capital of France and the proposition that there is a prime between every number greater than 1 and its double. Both are true, but the former could have been false and the latter could not have been false.

例如，对比命题“巴黎是法国首都”和命题“每个大于 1 的数字与其2倍数之间有一个素数”。两者都为真，但前者可能为假，而后者不可能为假。

Likewise, there is a distinction to be made within the class of false propositions: between those that could have been true and those that could not have been true (those that had to be false).

同样，在假命题的类中也可作出一个区分：「本可能为真」的命题和「不可能为真」的命题（即必然为假）。

Some Medieval philosophers supposed that the fact that true propositions are of the two sorts “necessarily true” and “contingently true” (and the corresponding fact about false propositions) showed that there were two “modes” in which a proposition could be true (or false): the mode of contingency and the mode of necessity—hence the term ‘modality’.

一些中世纪哲学家认为，真命题有“必然真”、“偶然真”2种类型。这个事实（以及关于假命题的相应事实）表明，一个命题为真有两种“模式”：偶然性模式和必然性模式。于是就有了“模态”这个术语。

Present-day philosophers retain the Medieval term ‘modality’ but now it means no more than “pertaining to possibility and necessity”.

当今的哲学家们保留了中世纪的术语“模态”，但现在它仅仅意味着“关于可能性和必然性”。

The types of modality of interest to metaphysicians fall into two camps: modality de re and modality de dicto.

形而上学家关注的模态类型可分为两个阵营：从物模态和从言模态。

Modality de dicto is the modality of propositions (‘dictum’ means proposition, or close enough). If modality were coextensive with modality de dicto, it would be at least a defensible position that the topic of modality belongs to logic rather than to metaphysics. (Indeed, the study of modal logics goes back to Aristotle’s Prior Analytics.)

“从言模态”是命题的模态（“dictum”意指命题，或意思足够接近）。如果模态与从物模态是共同外延的，那么「模态这一主题属于逻辑学而不是形而上学」，至少是一个站得住脚的立场。（事实上，对模态逻辑的研究可以追溯到亚里士多德的《前分析篇》。）

But many philosophers also think there is a second kind of modality, modality de re—the modality of things. (The modality of substances, certainly, and perhaps of things in other ontological categories.)

但许多哲学家也认为还有第二种模态，即从物模态——事物的模态。（当然包含实体的模态，也许还有在其它本体论范畴中事物的模态。）

The status of modality de re is undeniably a metaphysical topic, and we assign it to the “new” metaphysics because, although one can ask modal questions about things that do not change—God, for example, or universals—a large proportion of the work that has been done in this area concerns the modal features of changing things.

从物模态的地位毫无疑问地是一个形而上学的话题，我们将其归于“新”形而上学，因为尽管人们可以对不变之物（例如上帝或共相）提出模态问题，但在这一领域所做的很大一部分工作都涉及变化事物的模态特征。

There are two types of modality de re. The first concerns the existence of things—of human beings, for example.

从物模态有两种类型。第一个涉及事物的存在，例如人类的存在。

If Sally, an ordinary human being, says, “I might not have existed”, almost everyone will take her to have stated an obvious truth.

如果一个叫Sally的普通人，说，“我本可能不存在”，几乎每个人都会认为她陈述了一个显而易见的事实。

And if what she has said is indeed true, then she exists contingently.

如果她所言确实为真，那么她就偶然地存在。

That is to say, she is a contingent being: a being who might not have existed.

也就是说，她是一个偶然的存在：一个可能不存在的存在。

A necessary being, in contrast, is a being of which it is false that it might not have existed.

与此相反，一个必然的存在，就是说「它本可能不存在」是错误的。

Whether any objects are necessary beings is an important question of modal metaphysics.

是否有任何必然存在的对象，是模态形而上学的一个重要问题。

Some philosophers have gone so far to maintain that all objects are necessary beings, since necessary existence is a truth of logic in what seems to them to be the best quantified modal logic. (See Barcan 1946 for the first modern connection between necessary existence and quantified modal logic. Barcan did not draw any metaphysical conclusions from her logical results, but later authors, especially Williamson 2013 have.)

有些哲学家甚至坚持认为所有对象都是必然的存在，因为必然的存在是逻辑的真理，在他们看来，这是最好的量化模态逻辑。（参阅Barcan 1946，了解必然存在与量化模态逻辑之间的首个现代联系。Barcan未从她的逻辑结果中得出任何形而上学的结论，但后来的作者，尤其是Williamson在2013提出了。）

The second kind of modality de re concerns the properties of things.

第二种“从物的模态”涉及事物的性质。

Like the existence of things, the possession of properties by things is subject to modal qualification.

就像事物的存在一样，事物对性质的占有也是模态量化的主题。

If Sally, who speaks English, says, “I might have spoken only French”, almost everyone will take that statement to be no less obviously true than her statement that she might not have existed.

如果说英语的Sally说，” I might have spoken only French “，几乎每个人都会认为这句话的真实性不亚于她可能不存在的说法。

And if what she has said is indeed true, then “speaking English” is a property that she has only contingently or (the more usual word) only accidentally.

如果她所说的确实为真，那么“说英语”就是她contingently拥有的特性，或者（用更常见的词）是accidentally。

Additionally there may be properties which some objects have essentially.

此外，某些对象可能本质上具有特性。

A thing has a property essentially if it could not exist without having that property.

一个事物在本质上具有特性，如果它没有该特性就不能存在。

Examples of essential properties tend to be controversial, largely because the most plausible examples of a certain object’s possessing a property essentially are only as plausible as the thesis that that object possesses those properties at all.

本质特性的例子往往是有争议的，很大程度上是因为「某个特定对象本质上拥有某种特性的最合理」 的例证与「该对象拥有全部这些特性」的论点一样合理。

For example, if Sally is a physical object, as physicalists suppose, then it is very plausible for them to suppose further that she is essentially a physical object—but it is controversial whether they are right to suppose that she is a physical object.

例如，如果Sally是一个正如物理主义者所假设的物理对象，那么他们进一步假设她本质上是一个物理对象是非常合理的——但是他们假设「她是一个物理对象」是否正确是有争议的。

And, of course, the same thing can be said, mutatis mutandis, concerning dualists and the property of being a non-physical object.

那么当然，有关二元论者和作为非物理对象的特性，经适当修改，也同样可以这么说，

It would seem, however, that Sally is either essentially a physical object or essentially a non-physical object.

然而，从本质上来说，Sally要么是一个物理对象，要么是一个非物理对象。

And many find it plausible to suppose that (whether she is physical or non-physical) she has the property “not being a poached egg” essentially.

许多人认为，假设（无论她是物理的还是非物理的）她在本质上“不是荷包蛋”的特性是合理的。

The most able and influential enemy of modality (both de dicto and de re) was W. V. Quine, who vigorously defended both the following theses.

模态理论（包括从言模态和从物模态）最有能力和最具影响力的论敌是W. V.蒯因，他有力地捍卫了以下两个论点。

First, that modality de dicto can be understood only in terms of the concept of analyticity (a problematical concept in his view). Secondly, that modality de re cannot be understood in terms of analyticity and therefore cannot be understood at all.

首先，从言模态只能从分析性的概念（在他看来是一个有问题的概念）来理解。第二，从物模态不能从分析性的角度来理解，因此根本无法被理解。

Quine argued for this latter claim by proposing what he took to be decisive counterexamples to theories that take essentiality to be meaningful. If modality de re makes any sense, Quine contended (1960: 199–200), cyclists must be regarded as essentially bipedal—for “Cyclists are bipedal” would be regarded as an analytic sentence by those who believe in analyticity.

蒯因针对「本质是有意义的」理论给出了他认为是决定性的反例，并基于此来论证后一种论点。蒯因认为，如果从物模态是有意义的（1960：199-200），那么骑自行车的人必须从本质上被视为是双足的——因为“骑自行车的人是双足的”会被那些相信分析性的人视为一个分析句子。

But mathematicians are only accidentally bipedal (“Mathematicians are bipedal” is not analytic by anyone’s lights).

但数学家只是偶然地是双足的（任何人都不会认为“数学家是双足的”是分析性的）。

What then, Quine proceeded to ask, of someone who is both a mathematician and a cyclist?—that person seems both essentially and only accidentally bi-pedal. Since this is incoherent, Quine thought that modality de re is incoherent.

然后，蒯因继续发问，一个既是数学家又是骑自行车的人呢？这个人似乎既在本质上是双足的，又只偶然地是双足的。由于这是不融贯的，于是蒯因认为从物模态是不融贯的。

Most philosophers are now convinced, however, that Quine’s “mathematical cyclist” argument has been adequately answered by Saul Kripke (1972), Alvin Plantinga (1974) and various other defenders of modality de re.

然而，大多数哲学家现在确信，蒯因的“数学家自行车手”论点已经得到了索尔·克里普克（1972），阿尔文·普兰廷加（1974）和其他各种从物模态捍卫者的充分回答。

Kripke and Plantinga’s defenses of modality are paradigmatically metaphysical (except insofar as they directly address Quine’s linguistic argument).

克里普克和普兰廷加对模态的辩护在范式上是形而上学的（除了他们直接解决了蒯因的语言学论证）。

Both make extensive use of the concept of a possible world in defending the intelligibility of modality (both de re and de dicto).

两者都广泛使用了可能世界语义来捍卫模态的可理解性（包括从物模态和从言模态）。

Leibniz was the first philosopher to use ‘possible world’ as a philosophical term of art, but Kripke’s and Plantinga’s use of the phrase is different from his.

莱布尼茨是第一位使用“可能世界”作为艺术哲学术语的哲学家，但克里普克和普兰廷加对这个短语的使用与他的有所不同。

For Leibniz, a possible world was a possible creation: God’s act of creation consists in his choosing one possible world among many to be the one world that he creates—the “actual” world. For Kripke and Plantinga, however, a possible world is a possible “whole of reality”.

对莱布尼茨来说，一个可能世界是一个可能的造物：上帝的造世行为在于他从众多可能世界中，选择一个可能世界作为他所创造的世界，即现实世界。然而，对克里普克和普兰廷加来说，一个可能世界是一个可能的“现实整体”。

For Leibniz, God and his actions “stand outside” all possible worlds.

对莱布尼茨来说，上帝和他的行为“站在”所有可能世界之外。

For Kripke and Plantinga, no being, not even God, could stand outside the whole system of possible worlds.

对于克里普克和普兰廷加来说，没有任何存在，即使是上帝，可以站在整个可能世界的体系之外。

A Kripke-Plantinga (KP) world is an abstract object of some sort. Let us suppose that a KP world is a possible state of affairs (this is Plantinga’s idea; Kripke says nothing so definite).

一个Kripke-Plantinga（KP）世界是某种抽象对象。让我们假设KP世界是一种可能的状态（这是普兰廷加的想法；克里普克没有说得那么明确）。

Consider any given state of affairs; let us say, Paris being the capital of France.

考虑任何给定的事态；比如说，巴黎是法国的首都。

This state of affairs obtains, since Paris is the capital of France. By contrast, the state of affairs Tours being the capital of France does not obtain.

由于巴黎是 法国的首都，这种事态实现了。相比之下，图尔作为法国首都 的事态却没有实现。

The latter state of affairs does, however, exist, for there is such a state of affairs. (Obtaining thus stands to states of affairs as truth stands to propositions: although the proposition that Tours is the capital of France is not true, there nevertheless is such a proposition.)

然而，后一种事态确实存在，因为有这样一种事态。（因此，事态和实现的关系就像真理和命题的关系一样：尽管图尔是法国首都的命题为假，但仍然存在这样的命题。）

The state of affairs x is said to include the state of affairs y if it is impossible for x to obtain and y not to obtain. If it is impossible for both x and y to obtain, then each precludes the other.

如果事态 x 不可能实现而事态y 不可能不实现，则称事态 x 包括事态 y。如果 x 和 y 都不可能实现，则两种事态相互排除。

A possible world is simply a possible state of affairs that, for every state of affairs x , either includes or precludes x ; the actual world is the one such state of affairs that obtains.

一个可能世界只是一个可能的状态，对于每个事态x，它要么包含x，要么排除x ；现实世界就是这样一种实现的事态。

Using the KP theory we can answer Quine’s challenge as follows.

使用KP理论，我们可以这样回答蒯因的质疑。

In every possible world, every cyclist in that world is bipedal in that world. (Assuming with Quine that necessarily cyclists are bipedal. Apparently he had not foreseen adaptive bicycles.)

在每一个可能世界里，每一个骑自行车的人在该世界都是双足的。（假设蒯因认为骑自行车的人必然是双足的。显然，他没有预见到自适应自行车。）

Nevertheless for any particular cyclist, there is some possible world where he (the same person) is not bipedal.

然而，对于任何特定的骑自行车的人来说，都有一些可能世界，他（同一个人）不是双足的。

Once we draw this distinction, we can see that Quine’s argument is invalid.

一旦我们做出这种区分，我们就可以发现蒯因的论据是无效的。

More generally, on the KP theory, theses about de re essential properties need not be analytic; they are meaningful because they express claims about an object’s properties in various possible worlds.

更一般地说，在KP理论中，关于从物模态本质特性的命题不必是分析性的;它们是有意义的，因为它们表达了「关于对象在各种可能世界中的特性」的主张。

We can also use the notion of possible worlds to define many other modal concepts.

我们还可以使用可能世界的概念来定义许多其他模态概念。

For example, a necessarily true proposition is a proposition that would be true no matter what possible world was actual.

例如，一个必然为真的命题是一个命题，即无论什么可能世界是现实世界，都为真。

Socrates is a contingent being if there is some possible world such that he would not exist if that world were actual, and he has the property “being human” essentially if every possible world that includes his existence also includes his being human.

若有某个可能世界是现实世界，但苏格拉底却不存在，那么苏格拉底就是一个偶然的存在；如果在每个包含他存在的可能世界里，他都是人，那么他本质上就具有“是人”的特性。

Kripke and Plantinga have greatly increased the clarity of modal discourse (and particularly of modal discourse de re), but at the expense of introducing a modal ontology, an ontology of possible worlds.

克里普克和普兰廷加极大地提升了模态语义（尤其是从物模态）的清晰度，但代价是引入了模态本体论，即可能世界的本体论。

Theirs is not the only modal ontology on offer.

并不是只有他们提出模态本体论。

The main alternative to the KP theory has been the ‘modal realism’ championed by David Lewis (1986).

KP理论的主要竞争者是大卫·刘易斯（1986）主张的“模态实在论”。

Lewis’s modal ontology appeals to objects called possible worlds, but these “worlds” are concrete objects.

刘易斯的模态本体论诉诸于被称为可能世界的对象，但这些“世界”是具体的对象。

What we call the actual world is one of these concrete objects, the spatiotemporally connected universe we inhabit.

我们所说的现实世界就是这些具体对象之一，即我们所居住的时空相连的宇宙。

What we call “non-actual” worlds are other concrete universes that are spatiotemporally isolated from ours (and from each other).

我们所说的“非现实”世界是其他具体的宇宙，它们在时空上与我们（以及彼此之间）是隔离的。

There is, Lewis contends, a vast array of non-actual worlds, an array that contains at least those worlds that are generated by an ingenious principle of recombination, a principle that can be stated without the use of modal language (1986: 87).

刘易斯认为，有大量的非现实世界，一组至少包含由一个精巧的重组原理——这个原理可以无需使用模态语言来表述——所产生的那些世界（Lewis 1986: 87）。

For Lewis, moreover, “actual” is an indexical term: when I speak of the actual world, I refer to the world of which I am an inhabitant—and so for any speaker who is “in” (who is a part of) any world.

此外，对刘易斯来说，“现实的”是一个索引术语：当我谈到现实世界时，我指的是我所居住的世界——对于“在”（是一部分）任何世界的任何演讲者来说也是如此。

In the matter of modality de dicto , Lewis’s theory proceeds in a manner that is at least parallel to the KP theory: there could be flying pigs if there are flying pigs in some possible world (if some world has flying pigs as parts).

在从言模态问题上，刘易斯的理论在论述上至少与KP理论是相近的：如果某个可能世界中有飞猪（如果某个世界有飞猪作为部分），则可能存在飞猪。

But the case is otherwise with modality de re.

但在从物模态上，情况并非如此。

Since every ordinary object is in only one of the concrete worlds, Lewis must either say that each such object has all its properties essentially or else adopt a treatment of modality de re that is not parallel to the KP treatment.

由于每个普通对象都只存在于一个具体世界中，刘易斯就必须，要么说每个这样的对象本质上都有其所有特性，要么采用一种与KP理论不相同的从物模态处理方法。

He chooses the latter alternative. Although Socrates is in only the actual world, Lewis holds, he has ‘counterparts’ in some other worlds, objects that play the role in those worlds that he plays in this world.

他选择了后一种方案。刘易斯认为，虽然苏格拉底只存在于现实世界，但他在其他一些世界中也有“对应者”，这些对象扮演着他在这个世界的角色。

If all Socrates’ counterparts are human, then we may say that he is essentially human.

如果苏格拉底的所有对应者都是人类，那么我们可以说他本质上是人类。

If one of Hubert Humphrey’s counterparts won (the counterpart of) the 1968 presidential election, it is correct to say of Humphrey that he could have won that election.

如果休伯特·汉弗莱的对应者之一赢得了1968年的总统大选，那么汉弗莱说他本可以赢得那次选举是正确的。

In addition to the obvious stark ontological contrast between the two theories, they differ in two important ways in their implications for the philosophy of modality.

这两种理论之间除了有明显的本体论差异之外，它们对模态哲学的影响，也在两个重要方面有所不同。

First, if Lewis is right, then modal concepts can be defined in terms of paradigmatically non-modal concepts, since ‘world’ and all of Lewis’s other technical terms can be defined using only ‘is spatiotemporally related to’, ‘is a part of’ and the vocabulary of set theory.

首先，如果刘易斯是对的，那么模态概念可以用范式上的非模态概念来定义，因为“世界”和刘易斯的其他所有专业术语可以只使用“与时空相关的”、“是一部分的”和集合论的词汇来定义。

For Kripke and Plantinga, however, modal concepts are sui generis, indefinable or having only definitions that appeal to other modal concepts.

然而，对于克里普克和普兰廷加来说，模态概念是自成一格的，不可定义的，或者只有诉诸其他模态概念的定义。

Secondly, Lewis’s theory implies a kind of anti-realism concerning modality de re.

其次，刘易斯的理论暗含了一种关于从物模态的反实在论。

This is because there is no one relation that is the counterpart relation—there are rather various ways or respects in which one could say that objects in two worlds “play the same role” in their respective worlds.

这是因为没有一种关系是对应关系——有各种各样的方式或方面，人们可以说两个世界中的对象在各自的世界中“扮演着相同的角色”。

Socrates, therefore, may well have non-human counterparts under one counterpart relation and no non-human counterparts under another.

因此，苏格拉底很可能在一种对应关系下有非人对应者，而在另一种对应关系下没有非人对应者。

And the choice of a counterpart relation is a pragmatic or interest-relative choice.

对应关系的选择是一种实用主义的或利益相关的选择。

But on the KP theory, it is an entirely objective question whether Socrates fails to be human in some world in which he exists: the answer must be Yes or No and is independent of human choices and interests.

但根据KP理论，「苏格拉底在他存在的某个世界中是否不为人」是一个完全客观的问题：答案必须是“是”或“否”，并且独立于人类的选择和利益。

Whatever one may think of these theories when one considers them in their own right (as theories of modality, as theories with various perhaps objectionable ontological commitments), one must concede that they are paradigmatically metaphysical theories.

无论人们如何看待这些理论（如模态理论，如具有各种可能遭人反对的本体论承诺的理论），人们必须承认它们是范式上的形而上学理论。

They bear witness to the resurgence of metaphysics in analytical philosophy in the last third of the twentieth century.

它们见证了二十世纪下叶形而上学在分析哲学中的复兴。

3.2 Space and Time
3.2 空间与时间

Long before the theory of relativity represented space and time as aspects of or abstractions from a single entity, spacetime, philosophers saw space and time as intimately related. (A glance through any dictionary of quotations suggests that the philosophical pairing of space and time reflects a natural, pre-philosophical tendency: “Had we but world enough, and time …”; “Dwellers all in time and space”.)

早在「相对论将空间和时间描述为单一实体的方面或抽象形态」之前，哲学家们就认为空间和时间密切相关。（翻阅任何引文词典都会发现，空间和时间的哲学配对反映了一种自然的、前哲学的倾向：“如果我们的世界够大，时间够多……”；“所有存在于时空中的居民”。

Kant, for example, treated space and time in his Transcendental Aesthetic as things that should be explained by a single, unified theory.

例如，康德在他的《先验感性论》中将空间和时间视为应由单一的、统一的理论来解释的事物。

And his theory of space and time, revolutionary though it may have been in other respects, was in this respect typical of philosophical accounts of space and time.

他的空间和时间理论，尽管在其他方面可能是革命性的，但在这方面是典型的对空间和时间的哲学解释。

Whatever the source of the conviction that space and time are two members of a “species” (and the only two members of that species), they certainly raise similar philosophical questions.

无论「空间和时间是同一“种类”有且仅有的两个成员」这一信念从何而来，他们所引发的哲学问题显然是相似的。

It can be asked whether space extends infinitely in every direction, and it can be asked whether time extends infinitely in either of the two temporal “directions”.

我们可以追问空间是否可在每个方向上无限延伸，也可以追问时间是否可在两个时间“方向”上无限延伸。

Just as one can ask whether, if space is finite, it has an “end” (whether it is bounded or unbounded), one may ask of time whether, if it is finite, it had a beginning or will have an end or whether it might have neither, but rather be “circular” (be finite but unbounded).

正如人们可以追问，如果空间是有限的，那么它是否有一个“尽头”（无论它是有界的还是无界的），人们也可能会问时间，如果它是有限的，它是否有一个起点或将有一个终点，又或者它是否可能两者都没有，而是“循环”（有限但无界）。

As one can ask whether there could be two extended objects that were not spatially related to each other, one can ask whether there could be two events that were not temporally related to each other.

当人们问是否有两个广延对象在空间上互不相关时，也可以问是否有两个事件在时间上互不相关。

One can ask whether space is (a) a real thing—a substance—a thing that exists independently of its inhabitants, or (b) a mere system of relations among those inhabitants. And one can ask the same question about time.

人们可以问，空间是（a）一个真实的事物——一种实体——一个独立于其容纳物而存在的事物，还是（b）仅仅是其容纳物之间单纯的关系系统。关人们也可以就时间问同样的问题。

But there are also questions about time that have no spatial analogues—or at least no obvious and uncontroversial analogues.

但也有一些关于时间的问题在空间上没有类似的，或者至少没有明显且无争议的类似问题。

There are, for example, questions about the grounds of various asymmetries between the past and the future—why is our knowledge of the past better than our knowledge of the future?;

例如，关于过去和未来之间种种不对称缘由的问题——为什么我们对过去的知识优于我们对未来的知识？

why do we regard an unpleasant event that is about to happen differently from the way we regard an unpleasant event that has recently happened?;

为什么我们看待即将发生的不愉快事件的方式，与我们看待最近已发生的不愉快事件的方式不同？

why does causation seem to have a privileged temporal direction? There do not seem to be objective asymmetries like this in space.

为什么因果似乎有一个优先的时间方向？而在空间中似乎没有这样的客观不对称。

There is also the question of temporal passage—the question whether the apparent “movement” of time (or the apparent movement of ourselves and the objects of our experience through or in time) is a real feature of the world or some sort of illusion.

还有时间流逝的问题——时间的明显“运动”（或者我们自己和我们经验的对象通过时间或在时间上的明显运动）是世界的真实特征还是是某种幻觉？

In one way of thinking about time, there is a privileged temporal direction marking the difference between the past, present, and future.

在一种思考时间的方式中有一个优先的时间方向，标志着过去，现在和未来之间的差异。

A-theorists hold that time is fundamentally structured in terms of a past/present/future distinction.

A-理论家认为，时间从根本上说是根据过去/现在/未来的区别来建构的。

Times change from past to present to future, giving rise to passage. (The name ‘A-theorist’ descends from J.M.E. McTaggart’s (1908) name for the sequence past/present/future which he called the ‘A-series’.)

时代从过去到现在再到未来，从而产生了流逝。（“A-理论家”这个名字来自J.M.E.麦克塔加特（1908）对过去/现在/未来的序列的名称，他称之为“A-系列”。）

Within the A-theory, we might further ask whether the past and future have the “same sort of reality” as the present. Presentist A-theorists, like Prior 1998, deny that the past or future have any concrete reality.

在A-理论中，我们可能会进一步问，过去和未来是否与现在有“同样的实在性”。现在主义者的A-理论家，如普赖尔（1998），否认过去或未来有任何具体的实在性。

Presentists typically think of the past and future as, at best, akin to abstract possible worlds—they are the way the world was or will be, just as possible worlds are ways the actual world could be.

现在主义者通常认为过去和未来充其量类似于抽象的可能世界，它们是世界过去所是或将来所是的样子，正如可能世界是现实世界可能所是的样子。

Other A-theorists, like Sullivan (2012), hold that the present is metaphysically privileged but deny that there is any ontological difference between the past, present, and future.

其他A-理论家，如沙利文（2012），认为现在在形而上学意义上是优先的，但否认过去、现在和未来之间存在任何本体论差异。

More generally, A-theorists often incorporate strategies from modal metaphysics into their theories about the relation of the past and the future to the present.

更普遍地，A-理论家经常将模态形而上学的策略纳入他们关于过去和未来与现在关系的理论中。

According to B-theories of time, the only fundamental distinction we should draw is that some events and times are earlier or later relative to others. (These relations are called ‘B-relations’, a term also derived from McTaggart).

根据时间的B-理论，我们唯一能得出的根本区别是，某些事件和时间相对于其他事件和时间或早或晚。（这些关系被称为“B-关系”，这个术语也来自麦克塔加特）。

According to the B-theorists, there is no objective passage of time, or at least not in the sense of time passing from future to present and from present to past.

根据B-理论家的说法，不存在客观的时间流逝，或者至少不存在「从未来到现在，从现在到过去」这个意义上的时间流逝。

B-theorists typically maintain that all past and future times are real in the same sense in which the present time is real—the present is in no sense metaphysically privileged.

B-理论家通常认为，所有过去和未来的时间、和现在的时间是同样真实的——现在在形而上学意义上绝不是优先的。

It is also true, and less often remarked on, that space raises philosophical questions that have no temporal analogues—or at least no obvious and uncontroversial analogues.

同样，很少被提及的是，在空间上提出的一些哲学问题，在时间上没有类似的、或者至少没有明显和无争议的类似问题。

Why, for example, does space have three dimensions and not four or seven?

例如，为什么空间是三维的，而不是四维或七维的？

On the face of it, time is essentially one-dimensional and space is not essentially three-dimensional.

从表面上看，时间本质上是一维的，而空间本质上不是三维的。

It also seems that the metaphysical problems about space that have no temporal analogues depend on the fact that space, unlike time, has more than one dimension.

关于空间的形而上学问题在时间上没有类似的，似乎取决于这样一个事实：空间与时间不同，有不止一个维度。

For example, consider the problem of incongruent counterparts: those who think space is a mere system of relations struggled to explain our intuition that we could distinguish a world containing only a left hand from a world containing only a right hand.

例如，考虑一下「手性不对称物」问题：那些认为空间仅仅是一个关系系统的人，难以解释我们的直觉，即我们可以区分一个只包含左手的世界和一个只包含右手的世界。

So it seems there is an intuitive orientation to objects in space itself. It is less clear whether the problems about time that have no spatial analogues are connected with the one-dimensionality of time.

因此，空间中的对象似乎自身就有一种直觉上的方向。不太清楚的是，与空间问题缺乏类似性的关于时间的问题，这是否取决于时间的一维性。

Finally, one can raise questions about whether space and time are real at all—and, if they are real, to what extent (so to speak) they are real.

最后，人们还能追问，空间和时间是否实在——如果它们是实在的，它们又在多大程度上（会如此说）是实在的。

Might it be that space and time are not constituents of reality as God perceives reality but nevertheless “well-founded phenomena” (as Leibniz held)?

难道空间和时间不是现实（上帝感知的现实）的组成部分，而是“有充分根据的现象”（正如莱布尼茨所认为的那样）？

Was Kant right when he denied spatial and temporal features to “things as they are in themselves”?—and right to contend that space and time are “forms of our intuition”?

当康德否认空间和时间是“物自体”的特征时，他是对的吗？他还认为空间和时间是“我们直觉的形式”，这是对的吗？

Or was McTaggart’s position the right one: that space and time are wholly unreal?

还是麦克塔加特的立场是正确的：空间和时间是完全非实在的？

If these problems about space and time belong to metaphysics only in the post-Medieval sense, they are nevertheless closely related to questions about first causes and universals.

如果这些关于空间和时间的问题只属于后中世纪意义上的形而上学，那么它们仍然与第一因和共相问题密切相关。

First causes are generally thought by those who believe in them to be eternal and non-local.

第一因通常被那些相信它们的人认为是永恒的和非局部的。

God, for example—both the impersonal God of Aristotle and the personal God of Medieval Christian, Jewish, and Muslim philosophy—is generally said to be eternal, and the personal God is said to be omnipresent.

例如，上帝——无论是亚里士多德的非人格化上帝，还是中世纪基督教、犹太教和伊斯兰教哲学中人格化的上帝——通常被认为是永恒的，而人格化上帝据说是无所不在的。

To say that God is eternal is to say either that he is everlasting or that he is somehow outside time.

说上帝是永恒的，就是说他要么是永恒的，要么是在某种方式存在于时间之外的。

And this raises the metaphysical question of whether it is possible for there to be a being—not a universal or an abstract object of some other sort, but an active substance—that is everlasting or non-temporal.

这就提出了一个形而上学的问题，即是否有可能有这样一个存在——不是共相或是抽象的对象，而是一个主动实体——它是永恒的或非时间的。

An omnipresent being is a being that does not occupy any region of space (not even the whole of it, as the luminiferous ether of nineteenth-century physics would if it existed), and whose causal influence is nevertheless equally present in every region of space (unlike universals, to which the concept of causality does not apply).

无所不在的存在是一种不占据任何空间区域的存在（甚至不在空间全域内，如果它存在的话，就像十九世纪物理学假设的「光以太」），并且它的因果影响仍同等地出现于空间的每个区域（这一点与共相不同，因果概念不适用于共相）。

The doctrine of divine omnipresence raises the metaphysical question whether it is possible for there to be a being with this feature.

上帝无所不在的教义提出了一个形而上学的问题，即是否有可能有具有这种特征的存在。

Ante res universals are said by some of their proponents (precisely those who deny that universals are constituents of particulars) to have no relations to space and time but “vicarious” ones: the ante res universal “whiteness” may be said to be present where each white particular is, but only in a way analogous to the way in which the number two is present where each pair of spatial things is. ·

一些支持「共相先于对象存在」的人（正是那些否认共相是殊相组成部分的人）说，共相只是与空间和时间有一种“间接的”关系：先于对象存在的共相“白色性”可以说是存在于每个白色殊相所在的地方，但只是以一种类似于数字2存在于每对空间事物中的方式。·

But it is doubtful whether this is a position that is possible for a metaphysician who says that a white thing is a bundle composed of whiteness and various other universals.

但是，对于一个认为「白色的事物是由白色性和其他许多共相的“一束”」的形而上学家来说，这个立场的可能性是值得怀疑的。

Those who believe in the existence of in rebus universals are fond of saying, or have been in recent years, that these universals (‘immanent universals’ is a currently popular name for them) are “multiply located”—“wholly present” at each place at which the things that fall under them are present.

那些相信「共相存在于对象中」的人喜欢说（或者近年来一直喜欢这样说），这些共相（“内在共相”是目前流行的名称）是“多重定位”的 —— “完全存在”于「归入它们的事物存在的」每个地方。

And by this they certainly do not mean that whiteness is present in many different regions of space only vicariously, only as a number might be said to be present wherever there are things in that number, only in virtue of bearing the non-spatial relation “being had by” to a multitude of particulars each of which is present in a single region of space.

当然这不意味着，白色性只是间接地出现于许多不同的空间区域，或只是类似「一个数字可被说为出现在有该数的任何地方」，或只是具有与众多殊相（每个殊相都存在于一个空间的单个区域中）的非空间关系。

All theories of universals, therefore, raise questions about how things in various ontological categories are related to space. And all these questions have temporal analogues.

因此，所有关于共相的理论都提出了关于「各种本体论范畴内的事物如何与空间相关」的问题。以及所有这些问题都有在时间上的类似问题。

Persistence and Constitution
持存和建构

Related to questions about the nature of space and time are questions about the nature of objects that take up space or persist through time, and these questions form yet another central theme in post-medieval metaphysics.

与空间和时间的本质相关的问题是，关于「占据空间或随时间持存的对象的本质」的问题，这些问题构成了后中世纪形而上学的另一个中心主题。

Are some or all objects composed of proper parts?

部分或全部对象是由适当的部分组成的吗？

Must an object have proper parts in order to “fill up” a region of space—or are there extended simples?

一个对象必须有适当的部分才能“填满”一个空间区域，或者广延的简单个体是否存在？

Can more that one object be located in exactly the same region? Do objects persist through change by having temporal parts?

不止一个的对象可以位于完全相同的区域吗？对象是否因具有时间性部分而持存？

Much work on persistence and constitution has focused on efforts to address a closely knit family of puzzles—the puzzles of coincidence.

关于持存和建构的许多工作都集中在努力解决一个紧密相关的谜题家族上——重合谜题。

One such puzzle is the “problem of the statue and the lump”.

其中一个谜题是“雕像和碎块的问题”。

Consider a gold statue.

考虑一座金制雕像。

Many metaphysicians contend that there is at least one material object that is spatially co-extensive with the statue, a lump of gold.

许多形而上学家认为，至少有一个物质对象与雕像在空间上是同广延的，即一个金块。

This is easily shown, they say, by an appeal to Leibniz’s Law (the principle of the non-identity of discernibles). There is a statue here and there is a lump of gold here, and—if the causal story of the statue’s coming to be is of the usual sort—the lump of gold existed before the statue.

他们说，这很容易通过诉诸莱布尼茨定律（可辨别者的非同一性原则）来证明。这里有一座雕像，且也有一个金块，如果雕像存在的因由如通常情况那样，那么这个金块在雕像之前就已存在。

And even if God has created the statue (and perforce the lump) ex nihilo and will at some point annihilate the statue (and thereby annihilate the lump), they further argue, the statue and the lump, although they exist at exactly the same times, have different modal properties: the lump has the property “can survive radical deformation” and the statue does not.

即使上帝 无中生有地 创造了雕像（那必创造碎块），并将在某个时候湮灭雕像（从而湮灭碎块），他们进一步论证，雕像和碎块虽然存在于完全相同的时间，但具有不同的模态特性：碎块具有“可以在剧烈形变后存在”的特性，而雕像则没有。

Or so these metaphysicians conclude.

这些形而上学家总结的大概如此。

But it has seemed to other metaphysicians that this conclusion is absurd, for it is absurd to suppose (these others say) that there could be spatially coincident physical objects that share all their momentary non-modal properties.

但在其他形而上学家看来，这个结论是荒谬的，因为假设（之前那些形而上学家说的那样）存在空间上重合的物理对象，它们共享所有瞬时的非模态特性，这是荒谬的。

Hence, the problem: What, if anything, is the flaw in the argument for the non-identity of the statue and the lump?

因此，问题来了：如果有的话，雕像和碎块的非同一性论点的缺陷是什么？

A second puzzle in this family is the “problem of Tib and Tibbles”. Tibbles is a cat. Call his tail “Tail”.

这个家族的第二个谜题是“Tib和Tibbles问题”。Tibbles是只猫。称它的尾巴为“Tail”。

Call all of him but his tail “Tib”.

称它尾巴之外的部分为“Tib”。

Suppose Tail is cut off—or, better, annihilated. Tibbles still exists, for a cat can survive the loss of its tail.

假设“Tail”被切断了——或者更好的是，被湮灭了。Tibbles仍然存在，因为猫可以在失去尾巴后幸存下来。

And it would seem that Tib will exist after the “loss” of Tail, because Tib lost no part.

而且似乎Tib在“失去”Tail后仍存在，因为Tib没有失去任何部分。

But what will be the relation between Tib and Tibbles? Can it be identity?

但是Tib和Tibbles之间会有什么关系呢？它会是同一性吗？

No, that is ruled out by the non-identity of discernibles, for Tibbles will have become smaller and Tib will remain the same size.

显然不行，这为「可识别者的非同一性」所排除，因为Tibbles将变得更小，Tib将保持相同的大小。

But then, once again, we seem to have a case of spatially coincident material objects that share their momentary non-modal properties.

可如此一来，我们似乎有一个案例，物质对象空间上重合，且共享瞬时非模态特性。

Both these constitution problems turn on questions about the identities of spatially coincident objects—and, indeed, of objects that share all their (proper) parts. (A third famous problem of material constitution—the problem of the Ship of Theseus—raises questions of a different sort.)

这两个建构问题都指向了，关于空间上重合对象的同一性的问题，并且这些对象共有其全部（适当的）部分。（第三个著名的物质建构问题——忒修斯之船的问题——提出了另一种不同的问题。）

Some metaphysicians contend that the relation between the lump and the statue, on the one hand, and the relation between Tib and Tibbles, on the other, cannot be fully understood in terms of the concepts of parthood and (non-) identity, but require a further concept, a non-mereological concept, the concept of “constitution”: the pre-existent lump at a certain point in time comes to constitute the statue (or a certain quantity of gold or certain gold atoms that first constituted only the lump come to constitute them both); pre-existent Tib at a certain point in time comes to constitute Tibbles (or certain cat-flesh or certain molecules …). (Baker 2000 is a defense of this thesis.)

一些形而上学家认为，不论是碎块和雕像之间的关系，还是Tib和Tibbles之间的关系，从部分性和（非）同一性的概念上都无法充分表达清楚，而是需要一个更深入的概念，一个非部分论的概念，即“建构”的概念：预先存在的碎块在某个特定时间点构成了雕像（或者一定数量的金子或特定金原子，首先只构成碎块，进而构成两者）；预先存在的Tib在某个特定时间点构成Tibbles（或某些猫肉、某些分子……（Baker 2000对这一论点进行辩护。）

Others contend that all the relations between the objects that figure in both problems can be fully analyzed in terms of parthood and identity. For a more thorough overview of the solutions to these puzzles and different theories of constitution in play, see Rea (ed.) 1997 and Thomson 1998.

其他人则主张，在这两个问题中涉及到的对象之间的所有关系，都可以从部分性和同一性的角度进行充分的分析。有关这些难题的解决方案和现行的诸建构理论的更全面的概述，请参阅Rea 1997和Thomson 1998。

Causation, Freedom and Determinism
因果、自由和决定论

Questions about causation form yet a fourth important category of issues in the “new” metaphysics.

因果问题是“新”形而上学的第四个重要问题范畴。

Of course, discussion of causes go back to Ancient Philosophy, featuring prominently in Aristotle’s Metaphysics and Physics. But Aristotle understood ‘cause’ in a much broader sense than we do today.

当然，对原因的讨论可以追溯到古代哲学，这在亚里士多德的《形而上学》和《物理学》中占有突出地位。但亚里士多德对“原因”的理解比我们今天的更宽泛。

In Aristotle’s sense, a ‘cause’ or ‘aiton’ is an explanatory condition of an object—an answer to a “why” question about the object. Aristotle classifies four such explanatory conditions—an object’s form, matter, efficient cause, and teleology.

在亚里士多德的理解中，“原因”或“aiton”是一个对象的解释条件——是对这个对象的“为什么”问题的回答。亚里士多德将这四种解释条件进行了分类，即对象的形式因、质料因、动力因和目的因。

An object’s efficient cause is the cause which explains change or motion in an object.

一个对象的动力因是用来解释其变化或运动的原因。

With the rise of modern physics in the seventeenth century, interest in efficient causal relations became acute, and it remains so today.

随着十七世纪现代物理学的兴起，人们对动力因的兴趣变得浓厚，至今仍然如此。

And when contemporary philosophers discuss problems of causation, they typically mean this sense.

当代哲学家讨论因果问题时，他们通常是指动力因。

One major issue in the metaphysics of causation concerns specifying the relata of causal relations. Consider a mundane claim: an iceberg caused the Titanic to sink.

因果形而上学的一个主要议题，涉及明确因果关系的相关性。考虑一个通俗的说法：一座冰山导致泰坦尼克号沉没。

Does the causal relation hold between two events: the event of the ship hitting the iceberg and the event of the ship sinking?

两个事件（船撞上冰山的事件和船沉没的事件）之间的因果关系是否成立？

Or does it hold between two sets of states of affairs?

或是介于两组事态之间？

Or does it hold between two substances, the iceberg and the ship?

或是介于两个实体（冰山、船）之间？

Must causal relations be triadic or otherwise poly-adic?

因果关系必须是三元的吗？还是多元的？

For example, one might think that we are always required to qualify a causal claim: the iceberg, rather than the captain’s negligence, was causally responsible for the ship’s foundering.

例如，有人可能会认为，我们总需明确如下因果主张：冰山，而非船长的疏忽，为船沉没负因果责任。

And can absences feature in causal relations?

「缺乏」在因果关系中起作用吗？

For example, does it make sense to claim that a lack of lifeboats was the cause of a third-class passenger’s death?

例如，声称缺乏救生艇是某位三等舱乘客死亡的原因是否合理？

We might further ask whether causal relations are objective and irreducible features of reality.

我们可以进一步追问，因果关系是否是现实的客观和不可简化的特征。

Hume famously doubted this, theorizing that our observations of causation were nothing more than observations of constant conjunction.

休谟对此提出了著名的怀疑：理论上说，我们对因果的观察只不过是对恒常联结的观察。

For example, perhaps we think icebergs cause ships to sink only because we always observe ship-sinking events occurring after iceberg-hitting events and not because there is a real causal relation that holds between icebergs and foundering ships.

例如，也许我们认为冰山导致船舶沉没，只是因为我们总是观察到船沉没事件在在冰山撞击事件后发生，而不是因为冰山和沉没的船之间存在真正的因果关系。

Contemporary metaphysicians have been attracted to other kinds of reductive treatments of causation.

当代形而上学家已为「其他类型的因果还原性处理」所吸引。

Some—like Stalnaker and Lewis—have argued that causal relations should be understood in terms of counterfactual dependencies (Stalnaker 1968 and Lewis 1973).

有些人，如斯托内克尔和刘易斯，认为因果关系应该从反事实依赖的角度来理解（Stalnaker 1968和Lewis 1973）。

For example, an iceberg’s striking the ship caused its sinking at time t if and only if in the nearest possible worlds where the iceberg did not strike the ship at time t , the ship did not sink.

例如，在时间t冰山撞击船致其沉没，当且仅当在最近的可能世界中，冰山在时间t未撞中船，且船只未沉没。

Others have argued that causal relations should be understood in terms of instantiations of laws of nature. (Davidson (1967) and Armstrong (1997) each defend this view albeit in different ways.)

其他人则认为，因果关系应该从自然律的例示来理解。（Davidson（1967）和Armstrong（1997）各自捍卫了这一观点，尽管方式不同。）

All of these theories expand on an idea from Hume’s Treatise in attempting to reduce causation to different or more fundamental categories. (For a more complete survey of recent theories of causation, see Paul and Hall 2013.)

所有这些理论都详述了休谟论文中的一个观点，即试图将因果简化为不同或更基础的范畴。（有关近期因果理论的更完整考察，请参阅Paul and Hall 2013。）

Debates about causation and laws of nature further give rise to a related set of pressing philosophical questions—questions of freedom. In the seventeenth century, celestial mechanics gave philosophers a certain picture of a way the world might be: it might be a world whose future states were entirely determined by the past and the laws of nature (of which Newton’s laws of motion and law of universal gravitation served as paradigms).

关于因果和自然律的争论进一步引发了一系列相关的紧迫的哲学问题——自由问题。在十七世纪，天体力学给哲学家们描绘了一幅世界存在方式的某种可能的图景：它可能是一个未来状态完全由过去和自然律决定的世界（其中以牛顿的运动定律和万有引力定律为范式）。

In the nineteenth century the thesis that the world was indeed this way came to be called ‘determinism’.

在19世纪，世界确实如此的论点被称为“决定论”。

The problem of free will can be stated as a dilemma.

自由意志的问题可以说是一个两难的问题。

If determinism is true, there is only one physically possible future.

如果决定论是正确的，那么只有一个物理上可能的未来。

But then how can anyone ever have acted otherwise?

可如此一来，怎么会有人采取其他行动呢？

For, as Carl Ginet has said (1990: 103), our freedom can only be the freedom to add to the actual past; and if determinism holds, then there is only one way that the given—the actual—past can be “added to”.

因为，正如卡尔·吉内特所说（1990：103），我们的自由只能是增加到现实的过去之上的自由；如果决定论成立，那么只有一种方式可以“增加”给定的——现实的——过去。

But if determinism does not hold, if there are alternative physically possible futures, then which one comes to pass must be a mere matter of chance.

但是，如果决定论不成立，如果有其他物理上可能的未来，那么哪一种会发生，就只是一个偶然的问题。

And if it is a mere matter of chance whether I lie or tell the truth, how can it be “up to me” whether I lie or tell the truth?

如果我撒谎或说实话只是一个偶然的问题，那么我撒谎还是说实话，又怎么可能“取决于我”呢？

Unless there is something wrong with one of these two arguments, the argument for the incompatibility of free will and determinism or the argument for the incompatibility of free will and the falsity of determinism, free will is impossible.

我们有两个论证，一个是自由意志与决定论的不相容论证，另一个是自由意志与决定论的否定不相容的论证。除非这两个论证中的任何一个有问题，自由意志是不可能的。

The problem of free will may be identified with the problem of discovering whether free will is possible—and, if free will is possible, the problem of giving an account of free will that displays an error in one of (or both) these arguments.

自由意志的问题或许可以等同于发现自由意志是否可能的问题——以及，如果自由意志是可能的，那么在阐释自由意志的题时，会揭露上述两种论点之一（或两者都）的错误。

Van Inwagen (1998) defends the position that, although the modern problem of free will has its origin in philosophical reflections on the consequences of supposing the physical universe to be governed by deterministic laws, the problem cannot be evaded by embracing a metaphysic (like dualism or idealism) that supposes that agents are immaterial or non-physical.

Van Inwagen（1998）捍卫的立场是，尽管现代自由意志问题，起源于对假设物理宇宙受决定论定律支配的后果的哲学反思，但这个问题不能通过接受一个「假设行为主体是非物质或非物理的」形而上学（如二元论或唯心主义）来回避。

This leads into our next and final sample of topics from the “new” metaphysics.

这就引出了我们下一个也是最后一个“新”形而上学主题样本。

3.5 The Mental and Physical
3.5 心理和物理

If it is natural both to pair and to oppose time and space, it is also natural to pair and to oppose the mental and the physical.

如果说时间和空间的配对和比对是很自然的，那么心理和物理的配对和比对也是很自然的。

The modern identity theory holds that all mental events or states are a special sort of physical event or state.

现代同一性理论认为，所有心理事件或状态都是一种特殊的物理事件或状态。

The theory is parsimonious (among its other virtues) but we nevertheless exhibit a natural tendency to distinguish the mental and the physical.

这个理论是简约的（在它其他优点中），但我们仍然表现出一种自然的倾向，即区分心理和物理。

Perhaps the reason for this is epistemological: whether our thoughts and sensations are physical or not, the kind of awareness we have of them is of a radically different sort from the kind of awareness we have of the flight of a bird or of a flowing stream, and it seems to be natural to infer that the objects of the one sort of awareness are radically different from the objects of the other.

也许这样做的原因是认识论层面的：无论我们的思维和感觉是否是物理的，我们对它们的意识，与我们对鸟的飞行或溪流的流动的意识完全不同，并且似乎很自然地推断出，一种意识的对象与另一种意识的对象完全不同。

That the inference is logically invalid is (as is so often the case) no barrier to its being made.

这个推论在逻辑上是无根据的，（这是经常发生的情况），但这不妨碍该推论的提出。

Whatever the reason may be, philosophers have generally (but not universally) supposed that the world of concrete particulars can be divided into two very different realms, the mental and the material. (As the twentieth century passed and physical theory rendered “matter” an increasingly problematical concept, it became increasingly common to say “the mental and the physical”.)

无论出于什么原因，哲学家们通常（但不是普遍地）认为具体殊相的世界可以分为两个截然不同的领域，即心理的和物理的。（随着二十世纪的过去，物理理论使“物质”成为一个越来越有问题的概念，而说“心理和物理”变得越来越普遍。）

If one takes this view of things, one faces philosophical problems that modern philosophy has assigned to metaphysics.

如果一个人对事物持这种观点，他就将面临着现代哲学赋予形而上学的哲学问题。

Prominent among these is the problem of accounting for mental causation.

其中最突出的是解释心理因果的问题。

If thoughts and sensations belong to an immaterial or non-physical portion of reality—if, for example, they are changes in immaterial or non-physical substances—how can they have effects in the physical world?

如果思维和感觉属于现实的非物质或非物理的部分——例如，如果它们是非物质或非物理的实体——它们又是如何在物理世界中起作用的？

How, for example, can a decision or act of will cause a movement of a human body?

例如，一个决定或意志行为如何引起人体的运动？

How, for that matter, can changes in the physical world have effects in the non-physical part of reality?

就此而言，物理世界的变化如何对现实的非物理部分产生影响？

If one’s feeling pain is a non-physical event, how can a physical injury to one’s body cause one to feel pain?

如果一个人感到疼痛是非物理上的事件，那么身体的创伤又如何导致一个人感到疼痛呢？

Both questions have troubled “two realm” philosophers—or ‘dualists’, to give them their more usual name.

这两个问题都困扰着“两个领域”的哲学家，或者他们以一个更常用的名字，“二元论者”。

But the former has troubled them more, since modern physics is founded on principles that assert the conservation of various physical quantities.

但前一个问题使他们更加困扰，因为现代物理学建立在各种物理量守恒原理之上。

If a non-physical event causes a change in the physical world—dualists are repeatedly asked—does that not imply that physical quantities like energy or momentum fail to be conserved in any physically closed causal system in which that change occurs?

二元论者被反复追问：如果一个非物理事件引起了物理世界的变化，这是否意味着物理量，如能量或动量，在任何发生这种变化的物理封闭的因果系统中都无法守恒？

And does that not imply that every voluntary movement of a human body involves a violation of the laws of physics—that is to say, a miracle?

这是否意味着人体的每一次自主运动都涉及违反物理定律——也就是说，一个奇迹？

A wide range of metaphysical theories have been generated by the attempts of dualists to answer these questions.

二元论者试图回答这些问题，由此产生了许多形而上学理论。

Some have been less than successful for reasons that are not of much intrinsic philosophical interest. C. D. Broad, for example, proposed (1925: 103–113) that the mind affects the body by momentarily changing the electrical resistance of certain synapses in the brain, (thus diverting various current pulses, which literally follow the path of least resistance into paths other than those they would have taken).

有些之所以不太成功，是因为其理论中哲学意义不大。例如，C. D. 布罗德提出（1925：103-113），思维通过瞬间改变大脑中特定突触的电阻来影响身体（并严格沿着最小电阻路径而非其他方式来转移不同的电子脉冲）

And this, he supposed, would not imply a violation of the principle of the conservation of energy.

他认为，这并不意味着违反了能量守恒原理。

But it seems impossible to suppose that an agent could change the electrical resistance of a physical system without expending energy in the process, for to do this would necessitate changing the physical structure of the system, and that implies changing the positions of bits of matter on which forces are acting (think of turning the knob on a rheostat or variable resistor: one must expend energy to do this).

但是，似乎无法假设一个行为主义可以在不消耗能量的情况下改变物理系统的电阻，因为要做到这一点，就必须改变系统的物理结构，这意味着改变力所作用的物质粒子的位置（想想转动变阻器或可变电阻器上的旋钮： 人必须耗费能量才能做到这一点）。

If this example has any philosophical interest it is this: it illustrates the fact that it is impossible to imagine a way for a non-physical thing to affect the behavior of a (classical) physical system without violating a conservation principle.

如果这个例子有什么哲学意义的话，那就是：它说明了这样一个事实：在不违反守恒原理的条件下，我们无法想象一种非物理事物能影响（经典）物理系统的行为的方法。

The various dualistic theories of the mind treat the interaction problem in different ways.

各种心灵的二元论理论，以不同的方式处理相互作用问题。

The theory called ‘dualistic interactionism’ does not, of itself, have anything to say about the problem—although its various proponents (Broad, for example) have proposed solutions to it.

被称为“二元互动论”的理论本身对这个问题没有提供任何解决方案——尽管它的各种支持者（例如布罗德）已经提出了解决方案。

‘Occasionalism’ simply concedes that the “local” counterfactual dependence of the behavior of a physical system on a non-physical event requires a miracle.

“偶因论”只是承认，物理系统的行为对一个非物理事件的“局部”反事实依赖需要奇迹。

The theory of pre-established harmony, which substitutes “global” for local counterfactual dependence of voluntary physical movements on the mental states of agents, avoids problems with conservation principles—but secures this advantage at a great price. (Like occasionalism, it presupposes theism, and, unlike occasionalism, it entails either that free will does not exist or that free will is compatible with determinism.)

前定和谐理论认为，自主的物理运动对行为主体的心理状态的反事实依赖关系是“全体”的而非“局部”的，这就回避了守恒原理的问题——但以巨大的代价确保了这一优点。（如偶因论一样，它以有神论为前提，又与偶因论不同，它意味着自由意志要么不存在，要么与决定论兼容。）

‘Epiphenomenalism’ simply denies that the mental can affect the physical, and contents itself with an explanation of why the mental appears to affect the physical.

“副现象论”只是简单地否认心理可以影响物理，并满足于去解释为什么心理似乎会影响物理。

In addition to these dualistic theories, there are monistic theories, theories that dissolve the interaction problem by denying the existence of either the physical or the non-physical: idealism and physicalism. (Present-day philosophers for the most part prefer the term ‘physicalism’ to the older term ‘materialism’ for reasons noted above.)

除了这些二元论，还有一元论理论，这些理论——唯心主义和物理主义——通过否认物理或非物理的存在来消解相互作用问题。（由于上述原因，当今的哲学家大多更喜欢“物理主义”一词，而不是旧术语“唯物主义”。

Most current work in the philosophy of mind presupposes physicalism, and it is generally agreed that a physicalistic theory that does not simply deny the reality of the mental (that is not an “eliminativist” theory), raises metaphysical questions.

目前大多数心灵哲学的工作都以物理主义为前提，并提出了形而上学的问题，且广泛达成共识的是，物理主义理论并非简单否认心理的实在性（这不是“取消主义者”理论）。

Such a theory must, of course, find a place for the mental in a wholly physical world, and such a place exists only if mental events and states are certain special physical events and states.

当然，这样的理论必须在一个完全物理的世界中为心理找到一个位置，而且只有当心理事件和状态是某些特殊的物理事件和状态时，这种位置才会存在。

There are at least three important metaphysical questions raised by these theories.

这些理论至少提出了三个重要的形而上学问题。

First, granted that all particular mental events or states are identical with particular physical events or states, can it also be that some or all mental universals (‘event-types’ and ‘state-types’ are the usual terms) are identical with physical universals?

首先，假设所有殊相的心理事件或状态都与殊相的物理事件或状态相同，那么某些或所有心理共相（“事件类型”和“状态类型”是常用术语）是否与物理共相相同？

Secondly, does physicalism imply that mental events and states cannot really be causes (does physicalism imply a kind of epiphenomenalism)?

其次，物理主义是否意味着心理事件和状态不能真正成为原因（物理主义是否意味着一种副现象论）？

Thirdly, can a physical thing have non-physical properties—might it be that mental properties like “thinking of Vienna” or “perceiving redly” are non-physical properties of physical organisms?

第三，一个物理事物能否具有非物理特性——或许诸如“想起维也纳”或“感知为红”这样的心理特性，是物理生物体的非物理特性吗？

This last question, of course, raises a more basic metaphysical question, ‘What is a non-physical property?’

当然，最后一个问题提出了一个更基本的形而上学问题，“什么是非物理特性？”

And all forms of the identity theory raise fundamental metaphysical questions, ontological questions, questions like, ‘What is an event?’ and ‘What is a state?’.

所有形式的同一性理论都提出了基本的形而上学问题与本体论问题，诸如“什么是事件？”和“什么是状态？”。

The Methodology of Metaphysics
形而上学的方法论

As is obvious from the discussion in Section 3, the scope of metaphysics has expanded beyond the tidy boundaries Aristotle drew.

从第3节的讨论中可以明显看出，形而上学的范围已经超出了亚里士多德所划定的清晰界限。

So how should we answer our original question?

那么，我们应该如何回答我们最初的问题呢？

Is contemporary metaphysics just a compendium of philosophical problems that cannot be assigned to epistemology or logic or ethics or aesthetics or to any of the parts of philosophy that have relatively clear definitions?

当代形而上学是否只是一个「将无法归类于认识论、逻辑学、伦理学、美学，或者任何一个有明确定义的哲学领域的」哲学问题的纲要？

Or is there a common theme that unites work on these disparate problems and distinguishes contemporary metaphysics from other areas of inquiry?

或者是否有一个共同的主题，将这些不同问题的工作统一起来，并将当代形而上学与其他研究领域区分开来？

These issues concerning the nature of metaphysics are further connected with issues about the epistemic status of various metaphysical theories.

这些关于形而上学本质的问题与各种形而上学理论的认识论地位问题紧密相关。

Aristotle and most of the Medievals took it for granted that, at least in its most fundamental aspects, the ordinary person’s picture of the world is “correct as far as it goes”.

亚里士多德和大多数中世纪学者都认为，至少从最基本的方面来看，普通人对世界的图景是“就其本身而言正确的”。

But many post-Medieval metaphysicians have refused to take this for granted.

但许多后中世纪的形而上学家拒绝认为这是理所当然的。

Some of them, in fact, have been willing to defend the thesis that the world is very different from, perhaps radically different from, the way people thought it was before they began to reason philosophically.

事实上，他们中的一些人愿意捍卫这样一个论点：世界与人们开始哲学思辨之前的认识非常不同，也许完全不同。

For example, in response to the puzzles of coincidence considered in Section 3.3, some metaphysicians have maintained that there are no objects with proper parts.

例如，为了回应第3.3节中考虑的重合之谜，一些形而上学家坚持认为，具有适当部分的对象不存在。

This entails that composite objects—tables, chairs, cats, and so on—do not exist, a somewhat startling view.

这意味着复合对象——桌子，椅子，猫等——都不存在，这是一个有点惊人的想法。

And as we saw in Section 3.1, other metaphysicians have been happy to postulate the reality of concrete merely possible worlds if this posit makes for a simpler and more explanatorily powerful theory of modality.

正如我们在第3.1节中看到的，其他形而上学家很乐意假设具体可能世界的实在，只要这个假设可以使模态理论更简单、更具解释力度。

Perhaps this contemporary openness to “revisionary” metaphysics is simply a recovery of or a reversion to a pre-Aristotelian conception of a “permissible metaphysical conclusion”, a conception that is illustrated by Zeno’s arguments against the reality of motion and Plato’s Allegory of the Cave.

也许，当代这种对“修正”形而上学的开放，只是对前亚里士多德时代“允许的形而上学结论”构想的恢复或回归，芝诺反对运动实在的论点和柏拉图的洞穴寓言都有所说明。

But no matter how we classify it, the surprising nature of many contemporary metaphysical claims puts additional pressure on practioners to explain just what they are up to. They raise questions of the methodology of metaphysics.

但无论我们如何分类，许多当代形而上学主张的惊人本质都给实践者带来了额外的压力，要求他们解释他们到底在做什么。因此他们提出了形而上学方法论的问题。

One attractive strategy for answering these questions emphasizes the continuity of metaphysics with science.

对这些问题的一个有吸引力的策略，是强调形而上学与科学的连续性。

On this conception, metaphysics is primarily or exclusively concerned with developing generalizations from our best-confirmed scientific theories. For example, in the mid-twentieth century, Quine (1948) proposed that that the “old/intermediate” metaphysical debate over the status of abstract objects should be settled in this way.

根据这一构想，形而上学主要或完全关注「从我们最确凿的科学理论中」不断发展而来的普遍性判断。例如，在二十世纪中叶，蒯因（1948）提出，关于抽象对象地位的“旧/中级”形而上学辩论应该以如下方式解决。

He observed that if our best scientific theories are recast in the “canonical notation of (first-order) quantification” (in sufficient depth that all the inferences that users of these theories will want to make are valid in first-order logic), then many of these theories, if not all of them, will have as a logical consequence the existential generalization on a predicate F such that F is satisfied only by abstract objects.

他观察到，如果我们最好的科学理论用 “（一阶）量化的规范符号”来改写（要足够深入，使得这些理论的使用者想要做出的所有推论在一阶逻辑中都是有效的），那么这些理论中的许多理论，若非全部，都将产生一个逻辑结果，即对谓词F的存在概括，使得F仅由抽象对象满足。

It would seem, therefore, that our best scientific theories “carry ontological commitment” to objects whose existence is denied by nominalism. (These objects may not be universals in the classical sense. They may, for example, be sets.)

因此，似乎我们最好的科学理论，对那些被唯名论否认其存在的对象“进行了本体论的承诺”。（这些对象可能不是经典意义上的共相。例如，它们可能是集合。）

Take for example the simple theory, ‘There are homogeneous objects, and the mass of a homogeneous object in grams is the product of its density in grams per cubic centimeter and its volume in cubic centimeters’.

以简单的理论为例，“存在同质对象，一个同质对象的质量是它的密度（单位：克/立方厘米）和它的体积（单位：立方厘米）的乘积。”。

A typical recasting of this theory in the canonical notation of quantification is:

这一理论在经典量化符号中如下改写：

$$
\exists Hx \hspace{0.1cm}& \hspace{0.1cm}\forall x \hspace{0.1cm} (Hx→Mx= Dx × Vx)
$$
(‘Hx’: ‘x is homogeneous’; ‘Mx’: ‘the mass of x in grams’; ‘Dx’: ‘the density of x in grams per cubic centimeter’; ‘Vx’: ‘the volume of x in cubic centimeters’.)

（‘Hx’： ‘x 是同质的’；‘ Mx’：“x的质量（单位：克）”；‘ Dx’：“ x的密度（单位：克/立方厘米）”；‘Vx’： ‘x的体积（单位：立方厘米）’。）

A first-order logical consequence of this “theory” is：$\exists x \exists y \exists z (x=y×z)$

这个“理论”的一阶逻辑结果是：$\exists x \exists y \exists z (x=y×z)$

That is: there exists at least one thing that is a product (at least one thing that, for some x and some y is the product of x and y).

也就是说：至少存在一个事物，它是一个乘积（至少有一个事物，对于一些x和一些y来说，它是x和y的乘积）。

And a product must be a number, for the operation “product of” applies only to numbers. Our little theory, at least if it is recast in the way shown above, is therefore, in a very obvious sense, “committed” to the existence of numbers.

并且乘积必须是一个数，因为“乘”的运算仅适用于数字。因此，我们的小理论，至少如果它以上述方式改写，很明显地，对数字的存在“作出承诺”。

It would seem, therefore, that a nominalist cannot consistently affirm that theory. (In this example, the role played by ‘the predicate F’ in the abstract statement of Quine’s “observation” is played by the predicate ‘…=…×…’.)

因此，一个唯名论者似乎不能始终如一地肯定这一理论。（此例中，蒯因所“观察”的抽象陈述，“谓词*F’*的角色由谓词“…=…×…”扮演。）

Quine’s work on nominalism inspired a much broader program for approaching ontological questions.

蒯因在唯名论上的工作，启发了一个更广泛的处理本体论问题的方式。

According to “neo-Quineans”, questions about the existence of abstract objects, mental events, objects with proper parts, temporal parts, and even other concrete possible worlds are united to the extent that they are questions about the ontological machinery required to account for the truth of our best-confirmed theories.

根据“新蒯因主义”，关于「抽象对象、心理事件、具有适当部分的对象、时间片段，甚至其他具体可能世界」的存在的问题，都因它们是「关于解释我们最确信的理论的真实性所需的本体论机制的」问题而统一起来。

Still, many questions of the new and old metaphysics are not questions of ontology.

尽管如此，新旧形而上学的许多问题并非本体论问题。

For example, many participants in the debate over causation are not particularly worried about whether causes and effects exist.

例如，在因果的辩论中，许多参与者并不特别担心「原因和结果是否存在」。

Rather, they want to know “in virtue of what” something is a cause or effect.

相反，他们想知道“凭什么”某事是一个原因或结果。

Few involved in the debate over the mental and physical are interested in the question whether there are mental properties (in some sense or other).

参与关于心理和物理辩论的，很少有人对「心理特性（在各种意义上）是否存在」的问题感兴趣。

Rather, they are interested in whether mental properties are “basic” or sui generis—or whether they are grounded, partially or fully, in physical properties.

相反，他们感兴趣的是心理特性是“基本”的还是独特的，或者它们是否部分又或完全地基于物理特性。

Is there a unified methodology for metaphysics more broadly understood?

是否有一个统一的方法论来更广泛地理解形而上学？

Some think the task of the metaphysician is to identify and argue for explanatory relations of various kinds.

有些人认为形而上学家的任务是辨别和论证各种解释性关系。

According to Fine (2001), metaphysicians are in the business of providing theories of which facts or propositions ground other facts or propositions, and which facts or propositions hold “in reality”.

根据Fine（2001）的说法，形而上学家的工作是提供理论，以此说明哪些事实或命题是基于其他事实或命题的，以及哪些事实或命题“在现实中”成立。

For example, a philosopher might hold that tables and other composite objects exist, but think that facts about tables are completely grounded in facts about the arrangements of point particles or facts about the state of a wave function.

例如，某个哲学家可能认为桌子和其他复合对象存在，但认为关于桌子的事实完全基于「点粒子排列的事实或波函数状态」的事实。

This metaphysician would hold that there are no facts about tables “in reality”; rather, there are facts about arrangements of particles.

这位形而上学家会认为，“在现实中”没有关于桌子的事实；相反，有关于粒子排列的事实。

Schaffer 2010 proposes a similar view, but holds that metaphysical grounding relations hold not between facts but between entities.

Schaffer 2010提出了类似的观点，但认为形而上学的基础关系不是在事实之间，而是在存在体之间。

According to Schaffer, the fundamental entity/entities should be understood as the entity/entities that grounds/ground all others.

根据Schaffer的说法，那个（些）基础存在体应被理解为是其他所有存在体的基础。

On Schaffer’s conception we can meaningfully ask whether a table is grounded in its parts or vice versa. We can even theorize (as Schaffer does) that the world as a whole is the ultimate ground for everything.

根据Schaffer的构想，我们可以有意义地追问桌子是否以它的各个部分为基础，反之亦然。我们甚至可以得出这样的理论（就像Schaffer所做的那样）：世界作为一个整体，是万物的最终基础。

Another noteworthy approach (Sider 2012) holds that the task of the metaphysician is to “explain the world” in terms of its fundamental structure.

另一种值得注意的态度（Sider 2012）是，形而上学家的任务是根据其基本结构“解释世界”。

For Sider, what unites (good) metaphysics as a discipline is that its theories are all framed in terms that pick out the fundamental structure of the world.

对于Sider来说，把（好的）形而上学统一为一门学科的是，它的理论都是由「明确世界基本结构的」术语来构建的。

For example, according to Sider we may understand ‘causal nihilism’ as the view that causal relations do not feature in the fundamental structure of the world, and so the best language for describing the world will eschew causal predicates.

例如，根据Sider的说法，我们可以将“因果虚无主义”理解为因果关系在世界的基本结构中不起作用，因此描述世界的最佳语言因避免因果谓词。

It should be emphasized that these ways of delimiting metaphysics do not presuppose that all of the topics we’ve considered as examples of metaphysics are substantive or important to the subject.

应该强调的是，这些划分形而上学的方法，并不预设我们将其作为形而上学的例子所考虑的全部主题，都是实质性的或重要的。

Consider the debate about modality.

考虑一下关于模态的辩论。

Quine (1953) and Sider (2012) both argue from their respective theories about the nature of metaphysics that aspects of the debate over the correct metaphysical theory of modality are misguided.

蒯因（1953）和Sider（2012）都基于各自关于形上学本性的理论，认为某些模态形上学的争论搞错了方向。

Others are skeptical of the debates about composition or persistence through time.

其他人则对关于建构或随时间持存的辩论持怀疑态度。

So theories about the nature of metaphysics might give us new resources for criticizing particular first-order debates that have historically been considered metaphysical, and it is common practice for metaphysicians to regard some debates as substantive while adopting a deflationist attitude about others.

因此，关于形而上学本质的理论可能会给我们提供新的资源，来批判在历史上被认为是形而上学的特定一阶辩论，而形而上学家通常认为一些辩论是实质性的，而对另一些辩论采取紧缩态度。

Is Metaphysics Possible?
形而上学可能吗？

It may also be that there is no internal unity to metaphysics.

形而上学也许没有内在的统一性。

More strongly, perhaps there is no such thing as metaphysics—or at least nothing that deserves to be called a science or a study or a discipline.

更有力的说法是，也许没有形而上学这样的东西，或者至少它不值得被称为一门科学、研究或学科。

Perhaps, as some philosophers have proposed, no metaphysical statement or theory is either true or false.

也许，正如一些哲学家所提议的，没有一个形而上学的陈述或理论是非真即假的。

Or perhaps, as others have proposed, metaphysical theories have truth-values, but it is impossible to find out what they are.

或者，又如其他人所提出的，形而上学理论具有真理价值，但不可能找出它们是什么。

At least since the time of Hume, there have been philosophers who have proposed that metaphysics is “impossible”—either because its questions are meaningless or because they are impossible to answer.

至少自休谟时代以来，一直有哲学家提出形而上学是“不可能的”——要么是因为其问题毫无意义，要么是因为其问题不可能回答。

The remainder of this entry will be a discussion of some recent arguments for the impossibility of metaphysics.

本词条的最后部分将讨论近期关于形而上学不可能的一些论点。

Let us suppose that we are confident that we are able to identify every statement as either “a metaphysical statement” or “not a metaphysical statement”. (We need not suppose that this ability is grounded in some non-trivial definition or account of metaphysics.)

让我们假设，我们有信心能做到将每条陈述划定为“形而上学的陈述”或“不是形而上学的陈述”。（我们无需假设这种能力是基于「某种对形而上学的非平凡的定义或描述」上。）

Let us call the thesis that all metaphysical statements are meaningless “the strong form” of the thesis that metaphysics is impossible. (At one time, an enemy of metaphysics might have been content to say that all metaphysical statements were false. But this is obviously not a possible thesis if the denial of a metaphysical statement must itself be a metaphysical statement.)

让我们把「所有形而上学的陈述都是无意义的论点」，称为「形而上学是不可能的论点」的“强形式”。（曾经有一段时间，形而上学的论敌可能满足于说所有形而上学的陈述都是假的。但是，如果「对形而上学陈述的否定」本身必须是形而上学的陈述，那么这（形而上学论敌的）显然不是一个可接受的论点。）

And let us call the following statement the “weak form” of the thesis that metaphysics is impossible: metaphysical statements are meaningful, but human beings can never discover whether any metaphysical statement is true or false (or probable or improbable or warranted or unwarranted).

让我们把下面的陈述称为「形而上学是不可能的论点」的“弱形式”：形而上学的陈述是有意义的，但人类永远无法发现任何形而上学的陈述是真是假（或很可能的或不可能的，有根据的或无根据的）。

Let us briefly examine an example of the strong form of the thesis that metaphysics is impossible.

让我们简要地考察一下，「形而上学是不可能的论点」的强形式的例子。

The logical positivists maintained that the meaning of a (non-analytic) statement consisted entirely in the predictions it made about possible experience.

逻辑实证主义者坚持认为，一个（非分析性）陈述的意义完全在于它对可能经验所作出的预测。

They maintained, further, that metaphysical statements (which were obviously not put forward as analytic truths) made no predictions about experience.

他们进一步认为，形而上学的陈述（显然不是作为分析真理提出的）没有对经验做出任何预测。

Therefore, they concluded, metaphysical statements are meaningless—or, better, the “statements” we classify as metaphysical are not really statements at all: they are things that look like statements but aren’t, rather as mannequins are things that look like human beings but aren’t.

因此，他们得出结论，形而上学的陈述是无意义的——或者，更好的表述是，我们归类为形而上学的那些“陈述”根本不是真正的陈述：它们看起来像是陈述，但其实不是，就好比人体模型看起来像人，但其实不是人。

But (many philosophers asked) how does the logical positivist’s central thesis (The meaning of a statement consists entirely in the predictions it makes about possible experience) fare by its own standards?

但是（许多哲学家问道）逻辑实证主义者的中心论点（一个陈述的意义完全在于它对可能经验所作出的预测）以其自身标准来看会如何？

Does this thesis make any predictions about possible experiences?

这一论题是否对可能经验做出了任何预测？

Could some observation show that it was true?

有观察能证明这是真的吗？

Could some experiment show that it was false?

有实验可以证明它是假的吗？

It would seem not.

似乎不能。

It would seem that everything in the world would look the same—like this—whether this thesis was true or false. (Will the positivist reply that the offset sentence is analytic? This reply is problematic in that it implies that the multitude of native speakers of English who reject the logical positivists’ account of meaning somehow cannot see that that sentence is true in virtue of the meaning of the word “meaning”—which is no technical term but a word of ordinary English.)

不管这个论题是否成立，似乎世界上的一切仍旧是那个样子（或者大概这个意思）。（实证主义者会辩驳说，“补偿”的句子是分析性的吗？这个辩驳是有问题的，因为它暗示着，大量以英语为母语的人，他们拒绝逻辑实证主义者对“意义”的解释，因为“意义”这个词的含义，他们不知何故无法领会这句话是真的——这不是专业术语，而是一个普通的英语单词。）

And, therefore, if the statement is true it is meaningless; or, what is the same thing, if it is meaningful, it is false.

因此，如果陈述为真，那么它就毫无意义；或者，同样地，如果它有意义，那就为假。

Logical positivism would therefore seem to say of itself that it is false or meaningless; it would be seem to be, to use a currently fashionable phrase, “self-referentially incoherent”.

如此，逻辑实证主义似乎在说它本身是假的或无意义的；用目前流行的一个短语来说，似乎是“自我指涉地不融贯的”。

Current advocates of ‘metaphysical anti-realism’ also advocate a strong form of the thesis that metaphysics is impossible.

目前“形而上学的反实在论”的倡导者也在主张「形而上学是不可能的论点」的强形式。

Insofar as it is possible to find a coherent line of argument in the writings of any anti-realist, it is hard to see why they, like the logical positivists, are not open to a charge of self-referential incoherency.

即使在任何反实在论者的著作中都可以找到一条融贯的论据的情况下，也很难看出为什么他们和逻辑实证主义者一样，不接受自我指涉地不融贯性的指责。

Indeed, there is much to be said for the conclusion that all forms of the strong thesis fall prey to self-referential incoherency.

事实上，对于这一结论，即所有形式的强论点都成为「自我指涉地不连贯性」的牺牲品，还有很多可说的。

Put very abstractly, the case against proponents of the strong thesis may be put like this.

非常抽象地说，可以如下辩驳强论点的支持者：

Dr. McZed, a “strong anti-metaphysician”, contends that any piece of text that does not pass some test she specifies is meaningless (if she is typical of strong anti-metaphysicians, she will say that any text that fails the test represents an attempt to use language in a way in which language cannot be used).

McZed博士是一位“强反形而上学家”，她认为任何无法通过她指定测试的文本都是没有意义的（如果她是典型的强反形而上学家，她会说任何无法通过测试的文本都代表着，该文本试图以一种语言无法被使用的方式来使用语言）。

And she contends further that any piece of text that can plausibly be identified as “metaphysical” must fail this test.

她还进一步主张，任何能被合理视为“形而上学的”的文本必然无法通过测试。

But it invariably turns out that various sentences that are essential components of McZed’s case against metaphysics themselves fail to pass her test.

但事实一贯是，作为McZed反对形而上学实例中的基本组成部分的各种句子，也都未能通过她的测试。

A test-case for this very schematic and abstract refutation of all refutations of metaphysics is the very sophisticated and subtle critique of metaphysics (it purports to apply only to the kind of metaphysics exemplified by the seventeenth-century rationalists and current analytical metaphysics) presented in van Fraassen 2002.

在各种各样的对形上学的拒斥当中，范·弗拉森（2002）提出了一种对形而上学精巧细微的批判，为我们考察这种对形而上学的抽象拒斥提供了一个案例。（它声称只适用于十七世纪唯理论者和当前分析形而上学所举例的那种形而上学）。

It is a defensible position that van Fraassen’s case against metaphysics depends essentially on certain theses that, although they are not themselves metaphysical theses, are nevertheless open to many of the criticisms he brings against metaphysical theses.

一个可辩护的立场是，范·弗拉森反对形而上学的实例基本上针对某些特定的论点，虽然它们本身不是形而上学的论点，但仍然可以适用于他对于形而上学论点的批评。

The weak form of the thesis that metaphysics is impossible is this: there is something about the human mind (perhaps even the minds of all rational agents or all finite rational agents) that unfits it for reaching metaphysical conclusions in any reliable way.

「形而上学是不可能的论点」的弱形式是这样的：人类心灵（甚至可能是所有理性主体或所有有限理性主体的心灵）中的某些事物，使其无法以任何可靠的方式得出形而上学的结论。

This idea is at least as old as Kant, but a version of it that is much more modest than Kant’s (and much easier to understand) has been carefully presented in McGinn 1993.

这个想法至少可追溯康德，但是麦金恩在1993年已经审慎地提出了一个比康德更谦逊（也更容易理解）的版本。

McGinn’s argument for the conclusion that the human mind is (as a matter of evolutionary contingency, and not simply because it is “a mind”) incapable of a satisfactory treatment of a large range of philosophical questions (a range that includes all metaphysical questions), however, depends on speculative factual theses about human cognitive capacities that are in principle subject to empirical refutation and which are at present without significant empirical support.

然而，麦金恩认为「人类心灵（作为一种进化过程中的偶然产物，而不仅仅是因为它是“一个心灵”）无法令人满意地处理广泛的哲学问题（包括所有形而上学问题）」这一结论的论点，依赖于「关于人类认知能力的推测性事实的」论点，这些论点原则上受到经验的反驳，且目前没有重要的经验依据支撑。

For a different defense of the weak thesis, see Thomasson 2009.

有关弱论点的另一辩护，请参阅托马斯 2009。
